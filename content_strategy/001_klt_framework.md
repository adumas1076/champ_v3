## Why KLT Is The Core

The content wrapper (hook, structure, funnel stage, CTA, formatting, scoring) is HOW content gets made.

KLT is WHY content gets made. Every piece of content exists to move a stranger one step closer to buying. If a piece of content doesn't move someone from K→L, L→T, or T→Buy — it's noise.

**KLT is universal.** It works for a $100/mo community, a $2K/mo SaaS, a $50K consulting engagement, a $5 ebook. The stages don't change. The signals don't change. Only the CONTENT changes.

This is the system that every Cocreatiq client gets. Built on Anthony. Tested on Anthony. Proven by Anthony. Then templated for everyone.

## Stage 1: KNOW (Awareness)

### What It Means
The stranger discovers you exist. They see your face, hear your name, read your take. They don't know you yet — they're just aware you're in the room.

### The Job Of KNOW Content
- Stop the scroll
- Make them curious enough to click your profile
- Plant a seed: "this person might have something I need"
- Do NOT sell. Do NOT teach deeply. Just GET NOTICED.

### Content Types That Drive KNOW

| Format | Why It Works | Platform Sweet Spot |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Hot takes | Polarizing = shareable. People share opinions, not lessons. | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Quick tips (under 60s) | Low commitment. Easy to consume. Easy to share. | TikTok, Reels, Shorts |
| Contrarian statements | "Everyone says X. They're wrong." Stops the scroll. | Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok |
| Before/after reveals | Visual proof. Undeniable. | Instagram, TikTok |
| News + opinion | "X just happened. Here's why it matters." Rides trending waves. | Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Memes / humor | Lowest barrier. Highest share rate. | Twitter, Instagram, TikTok |
| Quote graphics | Snackable wisdom. Saves + shares. | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Duets / stitches | Piggyback on existing attention. | TikTok |
| Carousel openers | "5 things nobody tells you about X" — swipe bait. | Instagram, LinkedIn |

### KNOW Signals (How The System Detects Someone Is In KNOW)
- First-time profile visit
- First impression (saw content, no engagement)
- Single like or view (passive consumption)
- No follow, no comment, no save, no DM

### KNOW Metrics
| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Impressions | How many people SAW the content | Growth trend (up month over month) |
| Reach | Unique people who saw it | Wider = better |
| Profile visits | Curiosity signal — they clicked to learn more | 2-5% of impressions |
| New followers from content | KNOW → transitioning toward LIKE | Track per post |

### KNOW → LIKE Transition Trigger
**The person FOLLOWS you or comes back more than once.**

A follow means: "I want to see more of this." That's the signal that they've moved from "I know you exist" to "I want to keep watching." It's not LIKE yet — but it's the bridge.

Secondary triggers:
- Watched 3+ pieces of content in one session
- Saved a post
- Shared a post to their story/feed
- Commented for the first time

### KNOW Rules
1. Volume matters here more than anywhere. You can't be known if you're invisible.
2. Breadth over depth — reach new eyeballs, don't preach to the choir.
3. No teaching. No frameworks. No "let me break this down." Save that for LIKE.
4. Every KNOW piece should be consumable in under 30 seconds (text) or under 60 seconds (video).
5. The hook IS the content at this stage. If the hook doesn't hit, nothing else matters.

## Stage 2: LIKE (Affinity)

### What It Means
The stranger is now a follower (or repeat visitor). They've decided you're worth their time. Now they need to decide: "Do I LIKE this person? Do I vibe with their energy, their perspective, their style?"

LIKE is emotional. It's not about what you know — it's about WHO YOU ARE.

### The Job Of LIKE Content
- Show your personality, not just your expertise
- Make them feel something (laugh, think, nod, get fired up)
- Create "that's SO me" moments — identity resonance
- Build parasocial relationship — they feel like they know you
- Demonstrate that you THINK differently, not just that you know things

### Content Types That Drive LIKE

| Format | Why It Works | Platform Sweet Spot |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Stories (personal) | Vulnerability builds connection. "I've been where you are." | Instagram Stories, TikTok, YouTube |
| Behind-the-scenes | They see the REAL you. Process > polish. | Instagram, TikTok, YouTube |
| Opinions on trends | Not just news — your TAKE. Your lens. Your energy. | Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok |
| Day-in-the-life | Aspirational + relatable. "I want that life." | TikTok, Instagram, YouTube |
| Failures + lessons | "I lost $X" or "This flopped" — realness builds affinity. | All platforms |
| Reactions / commentary | Your personality applied to other people's content. | TikTok, YouTube, Twitter |
| Community shoutouts | Show you CARE about your people. Social proof + warmth. | Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn |
| Conversations / collabs | You + another creator = both audiences meet you. | YouTube, Twitter Spaces, IG Live |
| "Unpopular opinion" | Identity content. "If you agree with this, we're the same." | Twitter, TikTok, LinkedIn |

### LIKE Signals (How The System Detects Someone Is In LIKE)
- Consistent engagement (likes/comments on multiple posts)
- Replying to stories
- DM conversation (not purchase-related)
- Sharing your content with their audience
- Commenting with identity statements ("this is exactly how I think")
- Following for 7+ days with repeat engagement

### LIKE Metrics
| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Engagement rate | Are followers INTERACTING, not just watching? | 3-8% (platform dependent) |
| Comment quality | Are comments "great post" or real conversation? | Track meaningful comments |
| DM volume | People reaching out = high affinity | Growth trend |
| Story replies | Most intimate engagement signal on IG | Track weekly |
| Save rate | "I want to come back to this" = high value perception | 2-5% of reach |
| Share rate | "My friends need to see this" = identity match | 1-3% of reach |

### LIKE → TRUST Transition Trigger
**The person engages with your EXPERTISE content — not just your personality.**

They've moved from "I like this person" to "I think this person can actually help me." The signal: they start engaging with your educational, framework, or proof-of-work content instead of (or in addition to) your personality content.

Secondary triggers:
- Clicked a link in bio or CTA
- Downloaded a lead magnet
- Watched a long-form piece (8+ minutes)
- Asked a specific question in DMs ("how do you do X?")
- Attended a live session

### LIKE Rules
1. Be a person, not a brand. People like PEOPLE.
2. Your opinions are your differentiator. Generic = forgettable.
3. Show the mess, not just the wins. Perfection is unlikeable.
4. Consistency matters — show up in their feed enough that they feel like they know you.
5. LIKE takes time. You can't rush affinity. But you can accelerate it with vulnerability + consistency.

## Stage 3: TRUST (Authority)

### What It Means
They like you. Now they need to believe you can DELIVER. Trust is about proof, expertise, and demonstrating that you're not just entertaining — you're competent.

TRUST is logical. It's the head catching up to the heart.

### The Job Of TRUST Content
- Prove you know what you're doing (frameworks, breakdowns, case studies)
- Show results — yours AND your clients'
- Demonstrate depth — go beyond surface-level tips
- Answer their specific objections BEFORE they ask
- Make them feel safe: "If I give this person my money, I'll get value back"

### Content Types That Drive TRUST

| Format | Why It Works | Platform Sweet Spot |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Deep tutorials | "This person actually knows this inside and out." | YouTube, LinkedIn articles |
| Case studies | "They've done this for someone like me." | LinkedIn, YouTube, blog |
| Frameworks / systems | "They don't just do it — they have a SYSTEM." | Twitter threads, LinkedIn, YouTube |
| Screen recordings | "I can SEE them doing the work." Proof of competence. | YouTube, TikTok, Instagram |
| Client results | Social proof. "Real person, real result." | All platforms |
| Live builds / demos | Unedited proof of skill. Can't fake a live build. | YouTube, Twitch, IG Live |
| Long-form breakdowns | Depth = authority. 10-45 min videos signal expertise. | YouTube |
| Free tools / templates | "If the free stuff is this good, the paid stuff must be insane." | All platforms (link in bio) |
| Comparison content | "X vs Y — here's what I'd pick and why." Trusted advisor framing. | YouTube, Twitter, TikTok |
| "How I built X" | Process transparency. Shows thinking + execution. | YouTube, Twitter threads, LinkedIn |

### TRUST Signals (How The System Detects Someone Is In TRUST)
- Clicked CTA / visited landing page
- Downloaded lead magnet
- Signed up for email list
- Watched 70%+ of a long-form video
- Asked purchase-related questions in DMs ("how much is X?", "do you have a program?")
- Replied to nurture email
- Attended a webinar or live event
- Visited pricing page

### TRUST Metrics
| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Lead magnet downloads | They gave you their email = high trust | Track per asset |
| Email open rate | They're reading what you send | 30-50% |
| Email click rate | They're taking action from emails | 3-8% |
| Long-form watch time | They invest real time in your content | 50%+ average view duration |
| Landing page visits | They're exploring your offer | Track per CTA |
| DM purchase intent | They're asking about buying | Route to sales |
| Webinar attendance | High commitment = high trust | Track show-up rate |
| Pricing page views | They're seriously considering | Track per source |

### TRUST → BUY Transition Trigger
**The person takes a purchase-intent action.**

They've seen enough. They believe. Now they need a reason to act NOW. The signal: they visit a pricing page, ask "how do I start?", click a checkout link, or respond to a BOFU CTA.

Secondary triggers:
- Replied to email with purchase question
- Booked a call
- Started checkout (even if abandoned)
- DMed a purchase keyword (OPERATOR, BUILD, SCALE, BRAND, CREATE)

### TRUST Rules
1. Show, don't tell. "I'm an expert" = worthless. A 20-minute live build = undeniable.
2. Specificity builds trust. "I grew my business" = weak. "I went from $4K/mo to $47K/mo in 6 months using 3 operators" = strong.
3. Social proof compounds. Every testimonial, screenshot, and case study adds to the trust bank.
4. Answer objections proactively. If they're thinking "but what if X?" — address it in content before they ask.
5. Free value is the ultimate trust builder. Give away your best stuff. The right people will pay for implementation.

## Stage 4: BUY (Conversion)

### What It Means
They know you. They like you. They trust you. Now they need the offer, the CTA, and the reason to act NOW.

### The Job Of BUY Content
- Present a clear, compelling offer
- Remove friction (easy checkout, clear pricing, fast response)
- Create urgency (not fake scarcity — real reasons to act now)
- Handle the final objections (price, timing, "is this for me?")
- Make the buying experience feel GOOD, not sleazy

### Content Types That Drive BUY

| Format | Why It Works | Platform Sweet Spot |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|
| Direct offer posts | Clear: here's what you get, here's the price, here's how to start. | All platforms |
| Testimonial compilations | Stack social proof. Multiple voices saying the same thing. | Instagram, YouTube, landing page |
| "Who this is for" content | Self-selection. "If you're X, this is for you." | All platforms |
| Limited-time offers | Real urgency (cohort starts, price increase, bonus expires). | Email, DMs |
| Behind-the-scenes of client experience | "This is what it's like to be a client." Reduces uncertainty. | YouTube, Instagram |
| FAQ content | Address objections as content. Turns questions into conversions. | YouTube, blog, landing page |
| DM keyword triggers | "Comment OPERATOR and I'll send you the details." Low friction. | Instagram, TikTok, Twitter |
| Comparison to alternatives | "You could hire 3 people for $15K/mo, or use operators for $500/mo." | YouTube, LinkedIn |

### BUY Signals
- Clicked checkout link
- Started payment
- Completed payment
- Booked a call
- DMed purchase keyword

### BUY Metrics
| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Conversion rate | Leads → customers | 5-15% (warm), 1-3% (cold) |
| Revenue per content piece | Which content drives money? | DDO tracks this |
| CAC (cost to acquire) | What does it cost to get a customer? | < $100 |
| Time to purchase | How long from KNOW to BUY? | Track average journey length |
| Cart abandonment | Where do people drop off? | < 30% |

### BUY Rules
1. Don't sell to KNOW or LIKE people. They're not ready. You'll burn them.
2. Make buying easy. One click, one page, one action.
3. The offer should feel like the logical next step, not a pitch.
4. Urgency must be REAL. Fake countdown timers destroy trust.
5. Post-purchase experience matters. BAMFAM. Welcome gift. Immediate value.

## Stage 5: ADVOCATE (Loop)

### What It Means
The customer is so happy they become a marketer. They create KNOW for new strangers without you asking.

### Advocate Content (Created BY Customers)
- Testimonials (prompted and organic)
- Social posts about their experience
- Referrals to friends
- Community engagement that's visible publicly

### Advocate Triggers
- Prompted review request (Day 14, Day 30, Day 90)
- Referral program CTA
- Community challenges that generate public content
- "Tag someone who needs this" CTAs

### How Advocacy Creates KNOW
```
CUSTOMER posts about their experience
  → Their audience sees it (KNOW for new strangers)
  → New stranger clicks → enters YOUR KLT journey
  → LOOP
```

**This is why community is a moat. The members become the marketing.**

## The KLT Content Mix

### Mapping KLT to Funnel Stages

| KLT Stage | Funnel Stage | Content Mix | Goal |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|------|
| KNOW | TOFU | 50% of all content | Reach new people |
| LIKE | TOFU/MOFU | 30% of all content | Build connection |
| TRUST | MOFU | 15% of all content | Prove competence |
| BUY | BOFU | 5% of all content | Convert |

**Note:** The percentages are CONTENT MIX, not audience mix. Most of your content should create awareness because that's where the funnel is widest. You don't need much BUY content because by the time someone reaches BUY, they've consumed 20-50 pieces of your content already.

### KLT × Content Wrapper

Every piece of content uses the same wrapper (the 1-6 system) but the INTENT changes based on KLT stage:

| Wrapper Element | KNOW Intent | LIKE Intent | TRUST Intent | BUY Intent |
|----------------|-------------|-------------|--------------|------------|
| **1. Hook** | Curiosity / shock | Relatability / emotion | Problem / promise | Offer / result |
| **2. Structure** | Short, punchy | Story arc | Teaching framework | Objection → solution |
| **3. Funnel stage** | TOFU | TOFU-MOFU | MOFU | BOFU |
| **4. CTA** | Follow / share | Comment / save / DM | Download / subscribe / watch more | Buy / book / DM keyword |
| **5. Platform format** | Native to each | Native to each | Native to each | Native to each |
| **6. Eval score** | Reach + new follows | Engagement + saves | Leads + watch time | Conversions + revenue |

## KLT Journey Timeline (Typical)

| Stage | Duration | Content Touches | What's Happening |
|-------|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| KNOW | Day 1-3 | 1-3 pieces | "Hmm, who is this?" |
| KNOW → LIKE | Day 3-14 | 5-15 pieces | Following, starting to engage |
| LIKE | Day 14-30 | 15-30 pieces | "I vibe with this person." |
| LIKE → TRUST | Day 30-60 | 30-50 pieces | Engaging with deep content |
| TRUST | Day 60-90 | 50-80 pieces | Downloading, watching long-form, considering |
| TRUST → BUY | Day 60-120 | 60-100+ pieces | Takes a purchase action |

**At 12 pieces/day (3/day × 4 platforms), a single person can consume 360+ pieces in 30 days.** The timeline compresses with volume. This is why 3x/day matters — it accelerates KLT.

## KLT Detection System (For The Marketing Machine)

### How The System Knows Where Someone Is

| Signal | KLT Stage | System Action |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| First view, no engagement | KNOW | Serve more TOFU |
| Follow | KNOW → LIKE | Mix in personality content |
| Multiple engagements (3+ in 7 days) | LIKE | Serve stories, BTS, opinions |
| Comment with substance | LIKE | Flag for community invite CTA |
| Clicked bio link | LIKE → TRUST | Serve deep content, lead magnet CTA |
| Downloaded lead magnet | TRUST | Enter email nurture sequence |
| Watched long-form (70%+) | TRUST | Increase MOFU frequency |
| Visited pricing page | TRUST → BUY | Trigger retargeting + BOFU content |
| DMed purchase keyword | BUY | Route to Sales Operator |
| Completed purchase | CUSTOMER | Enter onboarding + advocacy loop |

### Scoring Model (Simplified)

```
KLT Score = K(awareness signals) + L(engagement signals) + T(intent signals)

K signals (1 point each): view, impression, profile visit
L signals (3 points each): follow, like, comment, save, share, story reply
T signals (10 points each): link click, lead magnet download, email open, long-form watch
B signals (25 points each): pricing page, DM keyword, checkout visit, call booked

Score ranges:
  0-10:   KNOW (serve TOFU)
  11-30:  LIKE (mix TOFU + personality)
  31-70:  TRUST (serve MOFU + deep content)
  71+:    BUY-ready (serve BOFU + route to sales)
```

## What This Means For Content Production

Every piece of content gets tagged:

```
content_metadata:
  klt_stage: KNOW | LIKE | TRUST | BUY
  funnel_stage: TOFU | MOFU | BOFU
  content_type: hot_take | story | tutorial | case_study | offer | ...
  platform: twitter | instagram | tiktok | linkedin | youtube
  cta_type: follow | comment | save | download | dm_keyword | buy
  hook_type: curiosity | contrarian | result | question | story
```

This metadata powers the eval system. The autoresearch loop learns which COMBINATIONS work best:
- "KNOW + hot_take + twitter + contrarian hook = 8.2/10 average"
- "TRUST + case_study + youtube + result hook = 9.1/10 average"
- "BUY + offer + instagram + dm_keyword CTA = 4.3% conversion"

**The system gets smarter with every post because it knows WHAT the content was trying to do, not just how it performed.**
