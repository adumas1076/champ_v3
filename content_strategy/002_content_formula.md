## What This Is

A repeatable, step-by-step formula that takes a business from zero content to a self-improving content machine. Every piece of content — every post, every video, every tweet — runs through this formula. No guessing. No vibes. System.

The formula has 12 steps. Every step has a clear input, a clear decision, and a clear output. The output of each step feeds the next.

**The formula in one line:**

```
Outlier → Slot → Bucket → Thread → Hook → Structure → CTA → Format → Test → Publish → Eval → Loop
```

## THE 12-STEP CONTENT FORMULA

---

### Step 1: OUTLIER RESEARCH

**What:** Find what's already winning in your niche. Don't guess what works — study what's PROVEN to work.

**Input:** Your niche, competitors, adjacent creators
**Output:** Patterns library (topics, hooks, structures, thumbnails, titles, lengths)

#### How To Find Outliers

An outlier is content performing **5-10x above** a creator's channel average. If someone averages 10K views and a video gets 80K — that's the one you study. The CONTENT did the work, not the brand.

**Where to find them:**
| Source | What To Look For |
|--------|-----------------|
| VidIQ / TubeBuddy | Outlier score filter — videos 5x+ above channel average |
| TikTok search | Sort by "most liked" in your niche hashtags |
| Twitter/X | High engagement relative to follower count |
| Instagram Explore | Content from small accounts that went viral |
| LinkedIn | Posts with 500+ comments from accounts with <10K followers |
| YouTube search | Filter by view count, find small channels with big videos |

**What to extract from each outlier:**

```
OUTLIER ANALYSIS TEMPLATE:
  Source URL: _______________
  Creator: _______________ (follower count: ___)
  Views/Engagement: _______________ (vs their average: ___)
  Outlier Score: ___x above average
  
  TOPIC: What is this about? _______________
  HOOK: What are the first 3 seconds / first line? _______________
  HOOK TYPE: curiosity | contrarian | result | question | story | shock
  THUMBNAIL: Describe it (face? text? colors? emotion?) _______________
  TITLE: Exact title _______________
  TITLE FORMULA: The pattern (e.g., "How I [RESULT] in [TIME]") _______________
  STRUCTURE: How is it organized? _______________
  LENGTH: _______________ 
  CTA: What do they ask the viewer to do? _______________
  WHY IT WORKED: Your analysis _______________
  WHAT TO STEAL: The transferable pattern _______________
```

**Frequency:** Weekly outlier research session. Minimum 10 outliers analyzed per week. Build a running library.

**The rule:** You're not copying content. You're copying PATTERNS. The topic is theirs. The structure is universal. The hook formula is transferable. Your voice and your value make it yours.

### Step 2: SLOT

**What:** Determine what the content calendar NEEDS right now. What funnel stage? Which face/persona?

**Input:** Content calendar, daily schedule, funnel balance tracker
**Output:** Assignment — funnel stage + face

#### Daily Slot System

| Time Slot | Funnel Stage | Content Goal |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Morning (8-10am) | TOFU | Value — attract new eyeballs |
| Afternoon (12-2pm) | TOFU | Engage — stories, opinions, personality |
| Evening (5-7pm) | MOFU | Deepen — CTA, lead magnet, DM trigger |

**Weekly BOFU:** 1-2x per week, replace one MOFU slot with BOFU (direct offer, testimonial, conversion content). Too early to hard sell with no audience — earn the right first.

#### Face/Persona Assignment

Every business has at least one face (the founder). Cocreatiq clients may have up to 4 (founder clone + AI faces).

```
FACE ASSIGNMENT DECISION:
  → What's the topic? → Which face owns that niche?
  → What's the platform? → Which face is primary on that platform?
  → What's the energy? → Founder for personal, AI faces for educational/tactical
```

**Output of this step:** "This is a TOFU post for the morning slot, assigned to [Face]."

|---|--------|-------------|----------|

| 3 | **Proof of Concept** | Show it works. Results. Receipts. Demos. | MOFU/BOFU, TRUST |
| 4 | **Overcoming Adversity** | Struggle stories. Failure → comeback. Resilience. | TOFU/MOFU, LIKE |

| 7 | **Community / Culture** | Showcase members, wins, belonging. Social proof. | MOFU/BOFU, TRUST |
| 8 | **Lifestyle / Vision** | Show the result of the path. Aspirational. | TOFU, KNOW |

#### Bucket-to-Funnel Mapping

```
TOFU slots → pull from: Contrarian, External, Mindset, Lifestyle, Adversity

**Output of this step:** "This is a TOFU / Contrarian post for [Face] in the morning slot."

### Step 4: THREAD CHECK

**What:** Determine if this post should be part of a running series or standalone.

**Input:** Post assignment from Steps 2-3, active series list
**Output:** Standalone OR connected to episode # of a series

#### The 3 Series Types

| Type | What It Is | Duration | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Saga** | Long-running narrative. No end date. The ongoing journey. | Indefinite | "Road to Generational Wealth" |
| **Series** | Fixed episodes. Beginning and end. Teaches something specific. | 5-12 episodes | "That's True But That's Not The Truth" |
| **Recurring** | Same format, new topic. Infinite. Becomes a ritual. | Weekly, same day | "Framework Friday" |

#### Series Rules

- **Target:** 20-30% of content should be episodic (connected to a series)
- **70-80%** remains standalone (the daily KLT machine)
- Every business starts with **at minimum 1 series** running
- Series content gets episodic tags: series name, episode #, narrative thread
- Series posts reference previous episodes ("Last week we talked about...")
- Each series has a **consistent visual identity** (same intro, same colors, same format)

#### Active Series Tracker

```
SERIES TRACKER:
  Series 1: _______________
    Type: Saga | Series | Recurring
    Face: _______________
    Frequency: ___x/week
    Current Episode: #___
    Status: Active | Paused | Complete
    
  Series 2: _______________
    ...
```

**Decision logic:**
```
Is there an active series that matches this slot's funnel stage + bucket + face?
  → YES: Make this the next episode. Connect to the thread.
  → NO: Standalone post. Move to Step 5.
```

**Output of this step:** "This is Episode 4 of 'True But Not The Truth' — TOFU / Contrarian / [Face]" OR "Standalone TOFU / Contrarian / [Face]"

### Step 5: HOOK

**What:** Write the first line (text) or first 3 seconds (video) that stops the scroll.

**Input:** Bucket, KLT stage, outlier patterns from Step 1
**Output:** The hook

#### The Hook Is Everything

- **65% of people** who watch the first 3 seconds will watch for 10+ seconds
- **Hook Rate** (3-sec plays / impressions) below 25% = algorithm suppresses your content
- The hook IS the content for KNOW-stage audiences. If the hook doesn't hit, nothing else matters.

#### 8 Universal Hook Types

| # | Hook Type | Formula | Best For |
|---|-----------|---------|----------|
| 1 | **Curiosity Gap** | "Most people don't know that [surprising thing]..." | KNOW, Education |
| 2 | **Contrarian** | "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong." | KNOW, Contrarian |
| 3 | **Result** | "I [achieved result] in [timeframe]. Here's how." | TRUST, Proof |
| 4 | **Question** | "Why do [group] always [frustrating thing]?" | KNOW, Mindset |
| 5 | **Story** | "3 years ago I was [bad situation]..." | LIKE, Adversity |
| 6 | **Shock / Pattern Interrupt** | "[Bold statement that breaks expectation]" | KNOW, Contrarian |
| 7 | **Challenge** | "I bet you can't [challenge] without [constraint]" | KNOW, Education |
| 8 | **Empathy** | "If you're [relatable situation], this is for you." | LIKE, Origin |

#### Hook-to-KLT Mapping

| KLT Stage | Best Hook Types | Why |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| KNOW | Curiosity, Contrarian, Shock, Question | Attention-first. Stop the scroll. |
| LIKE | Story, Empathy, Question | Connection-first. Feel something. |
| TRUST | Result, Challenge, Curiosity | Proof-first. Believe me. |
| BUY | Result, Empathy | Action-first. This is for you. |

#### Hook Rules

1. **No slow build-ups.** No "Hey guys, welcome back." No "So today I want to talk about." Death sentence for retention.
2. **The hook must create an information gap.** They need to feel like they MUST keep watching/reading to close the gap.
3. **Specificity wins.** "I made money" = weak. "I turned $47 into $12,000 in 6 weeks" = strong.
4. **Match the hook to the payoff.** If your hook promises something, the content MUST deliver it. Clickbait destroys trust.
5. **Test hook patterns from outlier research.** Don't invent — adapt what's proven to work.

#### Video-Specific Hook Additions

For video content (Reels, TikTok, Shorts, YouTube):
- **Visual hook** (first frame): face close-up, bold text overlay, unexpected image
- **Audio hook** (first 1 second): strong opening word, sound effect, music drop
- **Motion hook** (first 2 seconds): movement, zoom, cut — NOT a static shot

All 3 layers should fire simultaneously in the first 3 seconds.

**Output of this step:** The hook — first line or first 3 seconds — written and ready.

### Step 6: STRUCTURE

**What:** Build the body of the content following a proven framework.

**Input:** Hook from Step 5, bucket, platform, length constraints
**Output:** Full content structure (outline or draft)

#### Primary Structure: Hook → Lead → Meat → Payoff (Lamar)

| Section | What It Does | % of Content |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Hook** | Stop the scroll. Create the gap. (from Step 5) | 10% |
| **Lead** | Bridge from hook to substance. Set the frame. "Here's why this matters..." | 15% |
| **Meat** | The value. The framework. The story. The proof. The substance. | 60% |
| **Payoff** | Deliver on the hook's promise. Close the gap. Leave them better than you found them. | 15% |

#### Retention Techniques (Inside the Meat)

- **Retention phrases:** "But here's where it gets interesting..." / "Now watch what happens..." / "This is the part nobody talks about..."
- **The Every-5 Rule:** Something changes every 5 seconds in video (cut, angle, text, visual, b-roll). Prevents attention decay.
- **Nested loops:** Open a new curiosity gap inside the meat before closing the first one. Keeps them watching.
- **Specificity spikes:** Drop specific numbers, names, dates, results throughout. Vague = boring. Specific = credible.

#### SHORTS SCRIPTING FRAMEWORK (Primary — 90%+ of daily output)

Shorts are the engine. 3x/day × 4 platforms = almost everything is 15-60 seconds. This is the script formula for ALL short-form video content.

**The Formula: Hook → Tension → Payoff → Loop**

NOT Hook → Body → CTA. That's a lecture. This is a STORY — even in 30 seconds.

```
HOOK (0-3 seconds) — Stop the scroll
  ├── Visual: face close-up, bold text, unexpected image
  ├── Audio: first words spoken — no preamble, no "hey guys"
  ├── Text: on-screen text reinforcing the hook
  └── GATE: Must pass 3-second hold (65% who pass 3s watch 10s+)

TENSION (3-40 seconds) — Stretch the rubber band
  ├── Beat 1 (3-15s): First claim + proof/example
  ├── Beat 2 (15-25s): Escalation — "but here's the thing..."
  ├── Beat 3 (25-40s): Peak tension — setup for the payoff
  ├── Visual: cut every 2-4 seconds, never same shot >3 sentences
  └── Pacing: ~2 words/second (45s script = 90-110 words)

PAYOFF (last 3-5 seconds) — Deliver + close the gap
  ├── Must directly answer what the hook opened
  ├── Specificity wins ("$0 to $47K/mo" not "it worked out")
  └── If hook promises and payoff doesn't deliver = trust destroyed

LOOP (built into structure) — Make them rewatch
  ├── Narrative loop: ending recontextualizes the opening line
  ├── Visual loop: final frame matches opening frame
  ├── Replays count as additional views on ALL platforms
  └── Loop = strongest distribution signal in every algorithm
```

**Why Loop > CTA at the end:**
- Hard CTA at the end kills the loop (viewer stops instead of rewatching)
- Embed CTA in caption or payoff instead
- Replays (from loop) = 4-7x more impressions > one CTA click

**Shorts Script Template:**
```
SHORTS SCRIPT (15-60 seconds)

HOOK (0-3s):
  Visual: [What they SEE]
  Audio: "[First words — no intro]"
  Text: [On-screen text]

TENSION:
  Beat 1 (3-15s): [Claim + proof]
  Beat 2 (15-25s): [Escalation]
  Beat 3 (25-40s): [Peak tension → setup payoff]
  Visual notes: [Cuts, angles, B-roll]

PAYOFF (last 5s):
  "[Resolution — specific, satisfying, earned]"

LOOP:
  Narrative: [How ending → rehear opening?]
  Visual: [Final frame = opening frame?]

CTA: [In caption or embedded in payoff]
  Action: [ONE thing]

METADATA:
  Length: ___s | Words: ___ | Platform: ___
  KLT: ___ | Bucket: ___ | Series: ___
```

**Retention targets:**
| Metric | Target | What It Means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| 3-second hold | ≥65% | Hook is working |
| Completion rate | ≥70% | Tension + payoff are strong |
| Replay rate | ≥15% | Loop is working |
| Hook Rate (3s plays / impressions) | ≥25% | Algorithm will push it |

### Step 7: CTA

**What:** Attach one clear call-to-action matched to the KLT stage.

**Input:** KLT stage of the content, platform
**Output:** One CTA

#### The Rule: ONE CTA Per Piece

Multiple CTAs = no CTA. The audience should know EXACTLY what you want them to do next. One action. One next step.

#### CTA-to-KLT Mapping

| KLT Stage | CTA Goal | CTA Examples |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **KNOW** | Get them to come back | "Follow for more [topic]" / "Share this with someone who needs it" |
| **LIKE** | Get them to engage | "Comment [your take]" / "Save this for later" / "DM me your thoughts" |
| **TRUST** | Get them into your ecosystem | "Download the free [asset]" / "Link in bio" / "Subscribe for the deep dives" |
| **BUY** | Get them to take a purchase action | "DM me '[KEYWORD]'" / "Book your free call" / "Link in bio to start" |

#### Platform-Specific CTA Rules

| Platform | Best CTA Mechanic | Why |
|----------|-------------------|-----|
| Twitter | "Reply with..." or "Retweet if..." | Algorithm rewards replies and RTs |
| Instagram | "DM me [KEYWORD]" or "Save this" | DMs and saves are highest-signal actions |
| TikTok | "Comment [KEYWORD]" or "Follow for part 2" | Comments drive distribution |
| LinkedIn | "Share your experience in the comments" | Comments extend post reach significantly |
| YouTube | "Subscribe + bell" or "Watch this next" | Watch time > all other signals |

#### Keyword CTA System (For Lead Capture)

Pre-defined keywords that trigger automated responses:

```
KEYWORD TRIGGER MAP:
  Keyword: _______________
  Response: [Auto-DM with lead magnet + qualifying question]
  Routing: Hot (80+) → Sales | Warm (50-79) → Nurture | Cold (<50) → Drip
```

**Output of this step:** One CTA — clear, specific, matched to KLT stage and platform.

### Step 8: PLATFORM FORMAT

**What:** Adapt the content to be NATIVE to each platform.

**Input:** Full content draft from Steps 5-7
**Output:** Platform-specific version(s)

#### The Rule: Same Message, Native Delivery

The VALUE is the same. The FORMAT changes. What works on Twitter dies on TikTok. What works on LinkedIn sounds weird on Instagram. Reformat, don't just repost.

#### Platform Format Specs

| Platform | Format | Tone | Length | Visual |
|----------|--------|------|--------|--------|
| **Twitter/X** | Punchy. Line breaks. Each line = a thought. | Direct, opinionated, sharp | 280 chars or thread (5-10 tweets) | Optional image/video |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional but human. Storytelling. | Authoritative, vulnerable, insightful | 1,300-2,000 chars | Optional carousel or video |
| **Instagram Feed** | Visual-first. Caption supports the image. | Conversational, aspirational | 2,200 chars max, 125 before "more" | 1080×1350 (portrait) or 1080×1080 (square) |
| **Instagram Reel** | Fast-paced. Text overlays. Music/SFX. | Energetic, snappy | 30-60 seconds | 1080×1920 (vertical) |
| **TikTok** | Raw, authentic, fast. Talking head or screen recording. | Casual, direct, trend-aware | 15-60 seconds (up to 3 min for depth) | 1080×1920 (vertical) |
| **YouTube Long** | Produced. Chapters. B-roll. Depth. | Expert, comprehensive | 8-45 minutes | 1920×1080 (landscape) |
| **YouTube Short** | Fast hook. One idea. | Punchy, value-dense | 30-60 seconds | 1080×1920 (vertical) |

#### Cross-Platform Adaptation Rules

1. **Don't copy-paste across platforms.** Each version should feel like it was MADE for that platform.
2. **Lead with the platform's currency:**
   - Twitter = the take (opinion)
   - LinkedIn = the lesson (professional value)
   - Instagram = the visual (aesthetic + caption)
   - TikTok = the energy (raw + fast)
   - YouTube = the depth (comprehensive)
3. **Hashtags:**
   - Instagram: 3-5 relevant hashtags (niche > broad)
   - TikTok: 3-4 hashtags (trending + niche)
   - LinkedIn: 2-3 hashtags maximum
   - Twitter: 0-1 hashtags (less = better)
   - YouTube: Tags in metadata, not in description
4. **Captions/Subtitles:** ALWAYS on for vertical video (85% of social video is watched without sound)

**Output of this step:** Platform-native version(s) of the content, ready for review.

### Step 9: A/B TEST (High-Stakes Content Only)

**What:** Generate and test variations of hooks, thumbnails, and titles BEFORE committing.

**Input:** Formatted content from Step 8
**Output:** Winning variation selected

#### When To A/B Test

| Content Type | A/B Test? | Why |
|-------------|-----------|-----|
| Daily TOFU velocity posts | **No** | Speed > perfection. Volume teaches through eval. |
| MOFU lead magnet / deep content | **Yes** | Higher stakes. Worth optimizing pre-publish. |
| BOFU conversion content | **Yes** | Directly tied to revenue. Must perform. |
| YouTube pillar content | **Yes** | Thumbnail + title = 80% of the click. Test them. |
| Series episodes | **Test first 2-3, then lock format** | Find the winning format early, then repeat. |

#### What To Test

| Variable | Generate | Method |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Hook** (text) | 3 variations | Score against hook type criteria. Pick highest. |
| **Hook** (video) | 2 variations | Test different opening frames/words. |
| **Thumbnail** | 2-3 variations | Split test (YouTube native or TubeBuddy). |
| **Title** | 2-3 variations | Split test (YouTube native) or pre-score with CTR prediction. |

#### Pre-Score Method (When Split Testing Isn't Possible)

For platforms without native A/B testing, score variations against outlier patterns:

```
HOOK SCORE = 
  Curiosity gap strength (1-10)
  + Specificity (1-10)
  + Emotional trigger (1-10)
  + Pattern match to top outliers (1-10)
  / 4 = average score

Pick the highest scoring variation.
```

**Output of this step:** Best variation selected and locked for publishing.

```
PRE-PUBLISH CHECK:

### Step 11: EVAL (48 Hours Post-Publish)

**What:** Score the content's performance against benchmarks. Extract lessons.

**Input:** 48 hours of performance data from platform APIs
**Output:** Content score + lesson + pattern tags

#### Gate Metric: Hook Rate

**Before** running the weighted score, check:

```
HOOK RATE = 3-second video plays / total impressions

Hook Rate ≥ 25% → PASS → proceed to weighted scoring
Hook Rate < 25% → FAIL → flag for hook rewrite. Don't count weighted score.
                          The hook failed. Everything after it is irrelevant data.
```

*Hook Rate applies to video content only. For text posts, use "read more" click rate or engagement-to-impression ratio as the proxy gate.*

#### Weighted Content Score

```
CONTENT SCORE = weighted average of:

  Hook Score        (30%) — 3-sec retention (video) or scroll-stop rate (text)
  Retention Score   (25%) — watch-through / completion rate / time on post
  Engagement Score  (20%) — comments + shares + saves per view (NOT just likes)
  Conversion Score  (15%) — CTA click-through rate / DM keyword triggers
  Outlier Score     (10%) — performance vs YOUR channel average

Score ranges:
  9-10:  Outlier. Reverse-engineer why. Replicate the pattern.
  7-8:   Strong. This is working. Keep doing this.
  5-6:   Average. Fine but not moving the needle.
  3-4:   Underperforming. Identify what went wrong.
  1-2:   Failed. Kill this pattern. Don't repeat.
```

#### 3-Tier Benchmark Comparison

Every score is compared against 3 benchmarks:

| Benchmark | What It Tells You |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Your average** | Are you improving over time? |
| **Niche average** | Are you competitive in your space? |
| **Top 10% in niche** | What does great look like? How far away are you? |

#### Lesson Extraction

After scoring, extract ONE lesson per post:

```
LESSON TEMPLATE:
  Post: [link]
  Score: ___/10
  What worked: _______________
  What didn't: _______________
  Pattern tag: [hook_type + bucket + platform + klt_stage]
  Rule update: "When [condition], do [action] because [evidence]"
```

**Output of this step:** Score, benchmark comparison, lesson, pattern tags — all fed into the Loop.

### Step 12: LOOP

**What:** Feed lessons back into the system. The formula gets smarter every cycle.

**Input:** Lessons + pattern tags from Step 11
**Output:** Updated rules that improve Step 1 (Outlier patterns), Step 5 (Hook selection), Step 6 (Structure), and Step 3 (Bucket effectiveness)

#### The Autoresearch Loop

```
SCORED CONTENT (from Step 11)
  ↓
PATTERN ANALYSIS
  → After 50+ scored pieces: system identifies what combinations work
  → "Contrarian + Question hook + Twitter = 8.2/10 average"
  → "Education + Result hook + LinkedIn = 7.9/10 average"
  → "BTS + Story hook + TikTok = 6.1/10 average — underperforming"
  ↓
RULE GENERATION
  → System generates updated rules from patterns
  → "Increase contrarian content on Twitter (consistently high)"
  → "Avoid BTS on TikTok unless paired with a strong visual hook"
  → "Question hooks outperform curiosity hooks on LinkedIn by 1.4x"
  ↓
KNOWLEDGE UPDATE
  → Rules stored and applied to next content generation cycle
  → Outlier library updated with new winning patterns
  → Bucket rotation adjusted based on performance

  ↓

  → The system never stops learning
```
