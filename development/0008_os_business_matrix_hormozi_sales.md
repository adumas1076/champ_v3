## The 6 Elements of the Ultimate Sales Blueprint

### Element 1: Sales Multipliers (0:00 - 20:00)

Things OUTSIDE the sale that affect sales MORE than the sale itself.

**Multiplier 1: Sell 7 Days Per Week** [0:12]
- 52 weeks × 2 extra days = 104 more selling days = **29% revenue increase minimum**
- Weekends are when consumers are actually available (5pm-8pm especially)
- Leads who schedule on Saturday/Sunday for Monday = high no-show rate
- Same-day/next-day appointments have highest show-up rates
- *Source: Hormozi portfolio companies data, [0:12-3:12]*

**Multiplier 2: Respond to Leads in Under 1 Minute** [3:12]
- Contact within 60 seconds = **391% increase** in likelihood to close
- After 5 minutes, close likelihood **drops 80%**
- 50% of prospects go with the business that responds FIRST, not the best
- First impression = judgment cast over entire business
- *Source: Harvard Business Review case study + Allen (Hormozi's software company, 4000+ appointments/day), [3:12-7:00]*

**Multiplier 3: Increase Volume Per Closer** [8:06]
- Two ways to increase sales: hire more reps OR increase volume per rep
- Open more calendar slots = more throughput
- One rep doing 10 calls/day vs 5 = 2x revenue, same cost
- Opening calendars = strongest correlate to overall throughput (more than closing % improvements)
- *Source: Allen data, [8:06-15:00]*

**Multiplier 4: Show-Up Rate Optimization** [15:00]
- Shoutouts/leaderboards for closers who get highest show rates
- Confirmation sequences: text + email + call before appointment
- Reduce gap between booking and appointment (same day ideal)
- *Source: [15:00-20:00]*

### Element 2: Sales Training — Daily Huddles & Role Playing (20:02 - 37:40)

**Core Method: Daily Huddles with Real-Time Role Playing** [20:02]
- Role playing = the single greatest skill development tool for sales
- Set the frame: "No one's perfect, that's why we practice" [21:36]
- *Source: [20:02-22:00]*

**How to Role Play (Right vs Wrong)** [22:04]
- WRONG: Let them do full script, give 5 pieces of feedback at the end
- RIGHT: One piece of feedback at a time, in real-time
- Trainability is **inversely correlated** with feedback delay — faster feedback = fewer repetitions needed
- Stop them mid-sentence when they mess up, correct, continue
- Set expectation: "I'm going to stop you 30-40 times. That means we're doing it right." [23:54]
- *Source: Cross-species behavioral research cited by Hormozi, [22:04-25:00]*

**Game Tape Review** [29:00]
- Record sales calls, review them as a team
- Replay specific moments, discuss what worked and what didn't
- "Would you watch football without the replay?" — same for sales
- *Source: [29:00-32:00]*

**Three Pillars of Sales Performance** [30:37]
1. **Conviction** — belief in the product (can't fake this)
2. **Tone** — HOW you say it matters more than what you say
3. **Competence** — knowing the script and objection handles cold
- *Source: [30:37-37:00]*

### Element 3: The Pre-Sale — Sale Starts at the Click (37:40 - 50:00)

**The sale begins at the CLICK, not the call** [37:44]
- Everything between click and call affects close rate
- The ad they see, the landing page, the booking flow, the confirmation — all selling
- Continuity = seamless experience from first touchpoint to close
- *Source: [37:44-39:10]*

**Edifying the Closer** [39:26]
- Setter builds up the closer BEFORE the call: "Shawn is amazing, he's helped 400 people just like you"
- Specific examples: "He helped someone in your exact situation achieve X"
- Create urgency: "Let me see if he has an opening... yep, confirmed, 4pm works"
- vs. "Yeah someone will call you" = completely different frame
- *Source: Robert Cialdini's Influence + Hormozi, [39:26-41:00]*

**BANT Qualification** [41:01]
- IBM framework: **B**udget, **A**uthority, **N**eed, **T**iming
- Setter must confirm all 4 before passing to closer
- Unqualified leads waste closer's time and tank morale
- *Source: IBM study cited by Hormozi, [41:01-43:00]*

**Setter-Closer Handoff** [43:00]
- Setter stays on the line for warm handoff
- Setter recaps to closer IN FRONT of prospect: "This is Anthony, he's dealing with X, wants to achieve Y"
- Prospect hears their problem restated = they feel heard
- Closer starts from a position of knowledge, not cold
- *Source: [43:00-50:00]*

### Element 4: The CLOSER Framework — The Actual Sale (50:00 - 92:00)

**The CLOSER Acronym** [57:58]

| Letter | Step | What You Do | Timestamp |
|--------|------|-------------|-----------|
| **C** | Clarify | Clarify why they're here. "What made you reach out?" Remind them of the action THEY took. | [58:27] |
| **L** | Label | Label them with their problem. "So you're at X, you want to get to Y, and Z has been holding you back." Establish the gap. | [59:30] |
| **O** | Overview Past Pain | Walk through everything they've tried that didn't work. Quantify time, money, effort wasted. "The pain is the pitch." Deprivation creates motivation. | [60:10] |
| **S** | Sell the Vacation | Sell the OUTCOME, not the process. "We want to talk about Maui, not TSA." Three-pillar pitch: chunk your solution into 3 pillars, each with a metaphor. Pitch should be < 2 minutes. Spend 90% of time on prospect's pain, 10% on product. | [66:01] |
| **E** | Explain Away Concerns | Handle objections. AAA method: Acknowledge → Associate → Ask. "He who asks the most times gets the most sales." Maintain rapport so you get unlimited shots. Questions > statements. Be smoke — they can't disagree with a question. | [72:24] |
| **R** | Reinforce the Decision | Post-purchase handshake (not handoff). BAMFAM: Book A Meeting From A Meeting. CS person gets full context of pain/goals. Prevents drop-offs, cold feet, 30-day refunds. | [108:48] |

**Key Concepts Within CLOSER:**

**"The Pain is the Pitch"** [62:06]
- Pain cycle: deprivation creates motivation
- Quantify: how much they've spent, how long they've struggled, impact on their life
- The more pain they acknowledge, the more motivated they are to solve it
- *Source: [60:10-65:00]*

**Tone Guide** [55:58]
- Script tells you WHAT to say, tone guide tells you HOW to say it
- Different parts of the sale need different tones: curious, concerned, excited, matter-of-fact
- Emphasis, pacing, pauses — all scripted alongside words
- *Source: [55:45-57:50]*

**"Whoever Talks First After You Mention Price, Loses"** [102:14]
- State the price, then SILENCE
- Let them process
- Your instinct will be to fill the silence — don't
- *Source: [102:00-103:00]*

### Element 5: Objection Handling — Complete Framework (72:24 - 120:00)

**The AAA Method (Acknowledge → Associate → Ask)** [88:19]
- **Acknowledge**: Repeat back what they said. Buys time + builds rapport. "So it sounds like..."
- **Associate**: Link to something positive. "That's a smart question" or "Someone just like you asked that and got amazing results"
- **Ask**: Ask the next question. Move the sale forward. Person asking = person in control.
- This is what separates top salespeople from mediocre ones. [88:35]
- *Source: [88:19-92:00]*

**The Law of Sales** [90:39]
- "He who asks the most times gets the most sales" — this is a LAW
- But asking WRONG loses rapport → they stop talking to you
- AAA bridges this: it maintains rapport so you get unlimited shots on goal
- "The person asking is the person closing" [93:08]
- *Source: [90:39-94:00]*

**Rapid Fire Questions When They Say No** [77:02]
1. "What's your main concern?" (early in sale)
2. "What are you afraid of having happen?" (deeper)
3. "What are the variables you're using to make the decision?"
4. "What makes this a yes?"
5. "What makes this a no?" (most people are scared to ask this — don't be)
- *Source: [77:02-79:30]*

**Four Categories of Objection Closes** [104:14]

| Category | Root Cause | Close Strategy |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Time** | Prioritization problem | "It's not about time, it's about priorities. Will you ever NOT be busy?" [103:00] |
| **Money** | Value perception | "Nothing is too expensive, it's just not worth it yet. If I gave you a Ferrari for $5K, you'd find the money." [105:36] |
| **Decision Maker** | Authority delegation | 4-step: (1) "What if they said no?" → 1/3 say "I'd do it anyway" → close. (2) Ask their concern through the partner's mouth. (3) Past agreements. (4) "Support not permission." [113:48-119:00] |
| **Details/Preference** | Want your results their way | "You change the variables, you change the outcome." Question the detail, chunk up to the reason behind it. [82:06-88:00] |
| **Stall** | Decision avoidance / fear | Two root causes: fear of wrong decision (reassure) or don't know how to decide (walk them through it). [106:19] |

**All-Purpose Closes** [80:00]
- Work in ANY situation. Chunk up and zoom out.
- "Do you think the reason you're on the phone today is because you've struggled to make this decision in the past?" — works for money, time, spouse, anything [80:38]
- 1-to-10 Close: "Scale of 1 to 10, where are we? What would make it a 10?" [81:26]
- Best Case / Worst Case: "Best case you get X. Worst case you get your money back. Both risk-free. Which one?" [107:47]
- *Source: [80:00-82:30]*

**Details Are Death Traps** [82:29]
- Never give a "blind answer" — you're rolling the dice
- Always ask a question about their question: "Which days were you looking for?"
- Mechanic Close: "I can't ethically answer that without looking under the hood first" [83:54]
- Secretary Close: "That's like asking the secretary what's wrong with your heart" [83:46]
- The reason behind the reason: chunk up to WHY they care about the detail [86:37]
- *Source: [82:29-88:05]*

**Kill Zombies Early** [97:12]
- Handle predictable objections BEFORE asking for money
- Use BANT during the pain cycle (not during the close)
- Before money = you're a helpful guide. After money = you're extracting cash
- Be on the SAME SIDE of the table, literally and figuratively [98:33]
- *Source: [97:12-99:10]*

**When In Doubt, Say It Back** [99:07]
- If you blank, slowly repeat their question back: "So you want to know which certifications..."
- Buys time, keeps rapport, stays in the sale
- *Source: [99:07-99:50]*

**Before Getting Real, Get Permission** [100:00]
- "Can I be a coach instead of a friend here?"
- "Do you mind if I get real with you for a second?"
- "Would it be overstepping if I shared something uncomfortable?"
- If you lack authority (young seller to older buyer), use foils instead of direct truth
- *Source: [100:00-101:40]*

**Stack Closes** [101:52]
- When they're on the fence, don't wait — hit them with 2-3 closes from different angles
- "Don't memorize closes. UNDERSTAND the argument behind them." [104:14]
- You only need 2-3 closes per obstacle category that you're comfortable saying
- *Source: [101:52-107:20]*

**Childlike Curiosity** [91:47]
- When someone says no, your instinct should be curiosity not aggression
- "Huh, that's interesting. Tell me more." (head tilt up)
- Not competitive. Not attacking. Genuinely curious.
- "You want to understand, not win." [91:54]
- "You become rich by making THEM right. They believe everything they say, nothing you say." [92:46]
- *Source: [91:47-93:30]*

### Element 6: Sales Mindset & Behavioral Framework (126:44 - 153:58)

**Mindset 1: The Person Who Cares Most About the Prospect Wins** [126:57]
- If you truly care, you maintain rapport naturally
- Your tone and conviction change because you genuinely want to help
- Learn to TURN AWAY prospects — increases overall close rate + conviction
- "Commission breath" = prioritizing the close over the person. They feel it. You lose them.
- *Source: [126:57-128:00]*

**Mindset 2: Volume Negates Luck** [128:38]
- Sales is a numbers game. More volume = more skill = more volume (flywheel)
- Force-feed through the terrible period to reach mastery
- Skill curve: slow growth → plateau → exponential returns at the end. Most quit before the exponential.
- "Find the best closer on your team. Do double their effort."
- *Source: [128:38-131:00]*

**Mindset 3: Sales Resumes Don't Matter** [131:37]
- Group interviews > resume screening for transactional sales
- Hire for lowest skill deficiency (fastest to train)
- Attitude = bundled term for 20+ micro-skills. Sometimes easier to teach skills than attitude. Depends on role level.
- Lower roles: hire attitude, train skills. Higher roles: may need to hire skills, train culture.
- *Source: [131:37-135:00]*

**Mindset 4: Compensation Attracts, Doesn't Retain or Change Behavior** [135:01]
- Comp = recruiting tool, NOT performance tool
- Fast feedback loops change behavior more than comp changes
- One exception: compensate off CASH COLLECTED (not closes in Slack)
- Pro tip: Auto-zap from CRM processed sales to Slack (not self-reported closes)
- *Source: [135:01-136:56]*

**Mindset 5: Sales and Marketing Are One Continuum** [136:58]
- Put them under one roof (Chief Revenue Officer)
- Sales = personalized marketing — filling the info gaps marketing didn't cover
- If prospect needs 6 things to buy, marketing covers 1-3, sales covers 4-6
- The bigger the "plane" (price), the longer the "runway" (education) needed before they buy
- Multi-call closes = not enough pre-marketing. Fix the runway, not the pilot.
- *Source: [136:58-141:40]*

**Mindset 6: Say Less** [141:41]
- Prospect should have ~2/3 of talk time
- Let silence sit. People talk more when you don't fill the gap.
- Master interviewers (Steven Bartlett, etc.) let silence work for them
- *Source: [141:41-143:00]*

**Mindset 7: Don't Be Cute** [143:00]
- From a serial PE CEO: "Two fat guys in the middle, run to the right"
- Great salespeople NEVER skip the basics. Fundamentals every time.
- Prepare for calls, do warm handoffs, read notes, set agenda, follow script.
- "Doing the fundamentals is doing a lot of things over and over and never forgetting any steps."
- *Source: [143:00-144:50]*

**Mindset 8: No For Now ≠ No Forever** [145:00]
- Circle back every 3-6 months. Pulse check.
- Send fun memes / one-line emails to re-engage cold pipeline
- Obstacles may have been legitimate — timing changes
- *Source: [145:00-146:14]*

**Mindset 9: Be a Trashman / Yellow Equals Gold** [146:18]
- New closers should take ALL the trash leads. Free training on hard mode.
- "If I can close a homeless person, imagine what I can do with a qualified lead"
- Lead scoring: Green = hot, Yellow = warm, Red = cold. Yellows are gold for skill building.
- *Source: [146:18-148:55]*

**Mindset 10: Objections Don't Mean No** [149:02]
- "Wow that's expensive" ≠ "I don't want to buy"
- People voice reactions out loud. Doesn't mean it's an objection to overcome.
- Unless they explicitly say no, don't assume they're not buying
- Don't waste closes on observations — save them for real objections
- *Source: [149:02-150:07]*

**Mindset 11: Never Negotiate on Price** [150:07]
- "We could do it for more" — counter-anchor. Has never not worked. [150:40]
- If they see you lower price once, every price becomes negotiable forever
- Change TERMS (payment plans, feature downsells) not PRICE
- Test pricing ahead of time, never in the heat of a close
- *Source: [150:07-151:52]*

**Mindset 12: People Want to Buy** [151:54]
- People WANT to buy. They just have a history of being punished for buying.
- Your job: give them the excuse to justify the decision
- Be kind, not nice. Kind = genuinely help (even if uncomfortable). Nice = avoid conflict.
- Confront hard questions BEFORE asking for money, through foils
- Keep the human first. Always.
- *Source: [151:54-153:58]*

### Additional Frameworks From Previously Unread Sections

**Competition: Us vs Them (Not Us vs Us)** [27:49]
- Don't pit salespeople against each other internally
- Pick a common enemy (competitor): "Every customer we don't sell, they're selling"
- Internal competition creates toxic culture and loses good closers who thrive in team environments
- *Source: [27:49-28:54]*

**Game Tape: Sales + CS Together** [29:00]
- Weekly game tape review with BOTH sales AND customer success teams
- Sales sees what happens after they close → increases conviction + knowledge
- CS sees what sales promises → stops over-promise complaints
- Both teams improve: CS adds features sales asks for, sales sets better expectations
- This single change decreased post-sale drop-offs by 5-10%
- *Source: [29:00-32:00]*

**When You're Hot, Record Everything** [33:00]
- "Good closers try to get hot. Great closers never get cold."
- Record when you're on fire. Analyze: tone, pacing, transitions, timing of overcomes
- Systematize the "hotness" — turn it from luck into a repeatable process
- *Source: [33:00-34:13]*

**Reading Testimonials to Build Conviction** [34:22]
- When team is in a slump, read customer testimonials out loud
- Give kudos to the salesperson who closed the success story
- Highest close rates happened on "weigh-out day" — after seeing 20 people get real results, conviction was 100%
- "Sales is passing conviction from one person to another over a bridge of trust"
- *Source: [34:22-37:40]*

**Outbound Script: "Hey [Business], calling regarding [Competitor]"** [42:13]
- Voicemail/email: "Hey Tom, this is Alex. Calling in regards to Jones Dry Cleaning. Give me a call back."
- Creates extreme curiosity — they HAVE to call back
- Works for email and voicemail
- When they call back: "It looks like they're kicking your ass" or "We've reached out to them as well"
- *Source: [42:13-43:25]*

**Local Lead Calling Script** [44:05]
- "John?" (pause) — not "Is this John?"
- "This is Alex." (pause) — act like they should know you
- "You opted in on Facebook for our [specific promotion]" — three mental cues
- "I wanted to make sure you weren't a crazy person on the internet"
- "I've got 2:00 and 4:00 today, which works better?" — two options, both work for you
- *Source: [44:05-46:09]*

**5 Minutes of Prep Before Every Call** [46:49]
- Look up the person/business for 5 minutes
- "You'd be amazed how much of a genius you look like with 5 minutes of prep"
- Everyone else is so lazy that any effort looks amazing
- Never make the customer repeat themselves — YOU recap to THEM
- Notes pass from setter → closer → CS (seamless baton)
- *Source: [46:49-48:57]*

**Proof Promise Plan (Sales Opener)** [50:00]
- Open every sales call the same way content opens: Proof → Promise → Plan
- "John decided to get going with you on [big promise]. We've done this with lots of people like you. Here's the process. At the end we'll see if it makes sense. If not, no sweat."
- Takes 15-20 seconds. Sets the agenda. Shows you're a professional.
- "Skip the foreplay" — prospects respect directness
- *Source: [50:00-53:15]*

**Follow the F***ing Script** [53:36]
- Script adherence = the #1 thing to drill
- If everyone follows the script and nobody closes → script problem (one variable to fix)
- If nobody follows the script → can't fix anything
- First 2/3 of the sale = word for word. After the ask = loops and overcomes
- Script includes: emphasis (underline), tone (italics), pacing (bold), emphasis (CAPS)
- *Source: [53:36-57:58]*

**Anti-Guarantee Close** [123:06]
- When you can't offer a guarantee: "If you're the type who needs a guarantee, this isn't for you"
- "I can't eat the food for you. I can't work out for you. I can give you the guide."
- Takeaway sale — makes it for "serious people only"
- The less control you have over the outcome, the less you can guarantee
- *Source: [123:06-124:06]*

**"Let Me Go Through Your Free Stuff First" Close** [124:08]
- AGREE with them: "Absolutely, you should do that"
- Then: "One of two things will happen: you'll succeed and run into a new problem I can solve, or you won't succeed and I can solve that too. Either way, let's get started."
- *Source: [124:08-125:11]*

**Enterprise Sales: Multiple Bonds** [125:15]
- Transactional sales = one bond (easy to break)
- Enterprise = multiple people on both sides talking to each other
- More ties = harder to break = higher close rate
- Most of the CLOSER framework doesn't apply to 6-12 month committee decisions
- *Source: [125:15-126:43]*

## Operator SOP — Sales Operator (Built from Hormozi)

### Pre-Sale (Before the Call)
1. Respond to leads within 60 seconds
2. Edify the closer during booking
3. BANT qualify: Budget, Authority, Need, Timing
4. Warm handoff with recap
5. Available 7 days/week, prioritize evening/weekend slots

### During the Call — CLOSER Framework
1. **C** — "What made you reach out today?" Remind them of the action THEY took.
2. **L** — "So you're at X, wanting Y, and Z has been the blocker." Establish the gap.
3. **O** — "Walk me through what you've tried before." Quantify wasted time/money/effort. "The pain is the pitch." Deprivation creates motivation.
4. **S** — Three-pillar pitch (< 2 minutes). Statement + metaphor for each pillar. Sell the beach, not the plane ride. "What would it mean for your business if you could achieve Y?"
5. **E** — When they say no, use AAA: Acknowledge → Associate → Ask. Rapid-fire questions: "What's your main concern?" → "What are you afraid of?" → "What makes this a yes?" → "What makes this a no?" Use all-purpose closes. Kill zombies early (handle BANT before asking for money). Details = death traps (question the question, chunk up to reason behind it).
6. **R** — BAMFAM: Book A Meeting From A Meeting. Handshake not handoff. CS person gets full context. Edify the CS person the same way setter edified closer.

### Post-Sale
1. Reinforce immediately — "You made a great decision"
2. BAMFAM — schedule next touchpoint before hanging up
3. Warm handshake to CS/onboarding with full pain/goal context
4. Send welcome/onboarding within 5 minutes
5. Log: pain points, objections, resolution, close type — for team learning
