## Recurring Patterns Across All 10 Businesses

### Pattern 1: Fix the Model Before You Scale [39:00-42:00]
- If founder is maxed out at $90K/year, the model is broken
- Either 10x the price OR 10x delivery efficiency
- "Trying to scale what you're currently doing is a bad idea. Fix it first."
- Don't hire people to do more of a broken thing — fix the thing, then hire
- *Source: Cannabis agency founder, [39:00-42:00]*

### Pattern 2: The Three Legs of the Business Stool [44:13]
Every business has exactly three functions:
1. **Customer Acquisition** — get customers
2. **Customer Delivery** — serve customers
3. **Operations** — make sure nobody goes to jail (HR, finance, legal)
- If any leg is missing or weak, the stool falls
- *Source: [44:13-44:26]*

### Pattern 3: Diagnostic Selling > Package Selling [1:38-2:45]
- Don't sell 3/6/12 month packages
- Instead: diagnose the goal → calculate the timeline → price per week × weeks to goal
- "Sell the goal, not the program" — converts way higher
- Add guarantee: "If you don't hit the goal, I keep working with you until you do"
- *Source: Fitness business owner, [1:38-2:45]*

### Pattern 4: Add Revenue Lines With Zero Delivery Cost [3:03-4:10]
- Supplement affiliate sales on top of coaching = +50% revenue
- Don't sell on first visit — sell at 48-hour nutrition consult
- Assumed close: "Take these 3 supplements next to your coffee. Want to use the card on file?"
- Zero additional delivery cost, pure margin
- *Source: [3:03-4:10]*

### Pattern 5: Don't Stop What's Working to Start Something New [5:54-7:35]
- If Meta ads are producing 3:1 ROI, don't stop them to try LinkedIn
- Add the new channel ON TOP of the working channel
- Fix LTV first — turning 3:1 into 10:1 is better than finding a new channel
- "Diminishing returns at 10:1 means you go to 9:1. You're still killing."
- *Source: B2B lead gen business, [5:54-7:35]*

### Pattern 6: CEO Must Own Sales Knowledge [13:01-13:40]
- "You have a sales-driven business. You're the CEO. You should know how it works."
- If the sales director has leverage over you because you can't sell, you're hostage
- Take calls yourself → iterate the script → then teach it
- "You'll bet you cut half the script after 5 calls"
- *Source: Business with sales team turnover, [8:00-15:00]*

### Pattern 7: The Script Is the Bible [14:15-14:50]
- If nobody follows the script, training is irrelevant
- Script = word of God. Follow it perfectly.
- If someone follows the script perfectly and doesn't close = the SCRIPT needs work (not the person)
- This gives you ONE variable to optimize
- *Source: [14:15-14:50]*

### Pattern 8: Sales Training = Game Tape + Immediate Role Play [10:56-12:00]
- Watch recordings of people who sell well (game tape)
- Drill one aspect at a time with immediate correction
- "Say it again. Like this. Try again. Great. Keep going."
- Until they "breathe it" — can't do anything but say it right
- Then they can actually LISTEN on the call because they're not thinking about what to say next
- *Source: [10:56-12:00]*

### Pattern 9: Insult vs Criticism — The CEO Communication Framework [29:04-30:12]
- **Insult** = attack on character: "You suck"
- **Criticism** = discrepancy between stated and reality: "You said X, reality is Y"
- Criticism is an observation, not an attack
- Use this to have hard conversations with co-founders, employees, partners
- *Source: Agency co-founder problem, [29:04-30:12]*

### Pattern 10: You Don't Have Problems, You Need People [19:15-19:44]
- When you hear 6 different problems from a founder, the real problem is: you need help
- "You're missing three smart people"
- The first hire should be someone who can find their own salary in the business (CFO/controller)
- "Find your salary in 90 days. Then find the hole for the next hire."
- *Source: Used car dealership owner, [19:15-22:00]*

### Pattern 11: Hire CFO → Find Salary → Hire Next [20:37-21:14]
- CFO/controller hire pays for itself by finding waste
- "I almost guarantee you can find $250K/year from your top line vs bottom line"
- They find their salary, then they find the next hire's salary
- Each hire cascades into the budget for the next
- *Source: [20:37-21:14]*

### Pattern 12: Don't Promote Your Best Closer to Manager [8:08-12:32]
- "Top closer becomes sales manager — a song as old as time"
- Killers don't make good coaches. Different persona.
- Good sales managers are caring/attentive closers, not psychopath closers
- The killer was competition a week ago, now he's supposed to help them
- *Source: [8:08-12:32]*

## Operator SOPs Extracted

### Operations Operator — Business Diagnostic SOP
1. Ask: what are your top 3 problems?
2. Identify which one is actually limiting growth the most
3. Check: is it a model problem (broken economics) or a people problem (missing roles)?
4. If model problem → fix pricing or delivery efficiency first
5. If people problem → identify the 3 key hires needed
6. Prioritize: hire CFO first (they find their own salary)

### Sales Operator — Diagnostic Selling SOP
1. Don't present packages (3/6/12 month)
2. Diagnose the goal: "Where are you? Where do you want to be?"
3. Calculate: gap ÷ rate = timeline
4. Price: per week × weeks = total
5. Add guarantee: "If you don't hit the goal, I keep working with you"
6. Add zero-delivery upsell at second visit (supplements, add-ons)

### Lead Gen Operator — Channel Management SOP
1. Never stop a working channel to start a new one
2. Fix LTV on existing channel FIRST
3. Add new channels ON TOP of working ones
4. If ROI drops with more spend, fix LTV (not the ads)
5. Track: spend → leads → close rate → LTV → total ROI per channel
