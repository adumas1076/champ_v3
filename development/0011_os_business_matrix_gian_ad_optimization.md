## Framework: 7-Day Ad Optimization Cycle

### Step 1: Set Baseline Economics Before Launch [0:00-1:20]
- Know your ICP's cost per book call benchmarks:
  - B2C: <$50-$100 (cheap)
  - B2B: $200-$300 (average)
  - High-ticket/high-friction: $300-$500 (acceptable)
- Calculate your MAX cost per qualified book call for 2x ROAS
- "My max is $900 per qualified book call for a 2x. I'm completely fine if it's expensive."
- *Source: [0:00-1:20]*

### Step 2: Launch 10 Ads, 1 Ad Per Ad Set [5:04-5:19]
- ABO campaign (Ad Set Budget Optimization)
- 1 ad per ad set to expedite creative testing
- Equal budget across all ad sets ($16.66/day each = $166/day total = $5K/mo)
- Broad USA targeting, English, automatic placements for VSSL funnels
- *Source: [5:04-8:12]*

### Step 3: Don't Touch Anything for 7 Days [5:43-6:01]
- "I gave it 7 days before I even touched the ads"
- Let Meta overspend — "completely fine"
- Collect enough data for statistically valid decisions
- *Source: [5:43-6:01]*

### Step 4: Identify Winners and Losers at Day 7 [8:12-12:07]
- Sort by results (leads) descending
- Compare CPL to average (average was $34 in his case)
- Top 4 ads: clear winners (below average CPL)
- Bottom 4 ads: underperformers (CPL 50-78, above average)
- **BUT: Check last 3 days before killing** — look for ads that are improving
- "Video 30 is slowly picking up. CPL is below average in last 3.5 days. Keep it."
- *Source: [8:12-12:07]*

### Step 5: Kill Underperformers, Double Remaining Budgets [12:07-14:14]
- Turn off 4 underperforming ad sets
- Double the budget on remaining 6 ad sets ($16.66 → $33.32)
- "Since the budget's very low, I can double up. If you're spending $500/day per ad set, only do 20%."
- Scale rule: Low budget = can 2x. High budget = only +20%
- *Source: [12:07-14:14]*

### Step 6: Create BPA Campaign (Best Performing Ads) [18:10-24:04]
- Create a new CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) campaign
- Put ALL 6 winning ads into it
- Budget = remaining spend to hit target ($134/day to reach $333/day total = $10K/mo)
- **Use Post IDs** — carry over likes/comments/social proof from original ads
- Post ID = the unique string from the Facebook post URL
- "It's going to feed the data onto this ad even though it's a separate campaign"
- *Source: [18:10-24:04]*

### Step 7: UTM Everything [24:04-25:07]
- UTM tracking on every ad: campaign, ad set, ad level
- Track specific video code, ad copy code, and landing page
- Uses Zapier → Slack integration to see leads in real-time
- Cross-reference Facebook reported leads vs CRM actual leads
- *Source: [24:04-25:07]*

### Step 8: Update Creative Tracker [14:14-17:48]
- Spreadsheet with: code, theme, angle, format, launch date, file
- Mark each ad: Winner / Underperformer
- Analyze WHY winners won and losers lost:
  - Underperformers: "Hyper animation ads not performing. ICP callout + show-up-rates pain point did not do well."
  - Winners: "ICP callout + lead quality pain point is winning. Case study (low figure) is winning."
  - "Alex Hormozi-type animated belief-shifting ads are winning" (got lucky with Hormozi webinar timing, -50% CPL)
- Relay insights to creative team for next batch
- *Source: [14:14-17:48]*

### Step 9: Let It Rip Again [25:07-26:51]
- Don't touch for another 7-14 days
- "Expectations wise, CPL will fluctuate for 3-5 days. I don't even look at this."
- "It'll self-optimize and performance will get better"
- *Source: [25:07-26:51]*

## Operator SOP — Ad Optimization Operator

### Weekly Cycle (Every 7 Days)
1. Pull 7-day data from ad account
2. Calculate average CPL across all ads
3. Sort ads by results (leads) descending
4. Identify winners (below-average CPL) and losers (above-average CPL)
5. Check last 3 days on losers — look for improving trends before killing
6. Kill confirmed underperformers
7. Double budget on winners (if budget <$100/day per ad set) or +20% (if >$100/day)
8. Create BPA campaign with winning post IDs + remaining budget
9. Update creative tracker spreadsheet (winner/loser/theme/angle)
10. Relay insights to creative team: "More of X angle. Stop making Y type."
11. Don't touch for 7 more days

### Creative Feedback Loop
1. Track naming convention: VV (video), IMG (image), C (carousel), GIF
2. Each ad has a code (VV29, VV36, etc.) matching creative tracker
3. When ad wins/loses, update tracker and relay to creative team
4. Analyze themes: "ICP callout + lead quality pain = winning. Animation + show-up-rate pain = losing."
5. Next batch: 3-5 new variations of winning themes
