## Framework 1: The Offer Equation (Creative Strategy) [6:43-10:32]

The equation for ad scripts/copy that produces results:

```
Market Callout → Pain Point → Solution → Introduction → Social Proof (Case Studies)
→ USP (Unique Selling Proposition) → Guarantee (optional) → Urgency → CTA
```

| Component | What It Is | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Market Callout** | Name your ICP directly | "Coaches and consultants..." |
| **Pain Point** | Their #1 struggle | "...struggling to fill your calendar" |
| **Solution** | Your method (branded) | "We've created a new method that generates 100+ qualified appointments/mo" |
| **Social Proof** | 3 case studies (MAX 3 in ads) | "John went from $10K to $75K/mo in 6 months" (repeat 3x with different clients) |
| **USP / Unique Mechanism** | Sexy name for your deliverable | "AI Sales Agents" / "Digital Road Maps" / "Geo-Targeted Referrals" |
| **Guarantee** | Risk reversal (optional in 2024) | "100 book calls in 30 days or your money back" |
| **Urgency** | Real deadline, NOT fake timers | "This offer ends December 31st" — specific real date on the ad |
| **CTA** | Where to go | Book a call / watch VSL |

**Key insight on guarantees** [9:46-10:04]:
- Saturated markets: stray AWAY from guarantees
- New markets: guarantees help a lot
- Always split test with/without

**Key insight on urgency** [10:12-10:32]:
- Real deadlines with specific end dates convert much higher
- Fake countdown timers = "complete shit. I've spent a lot of money on mistakes with those." [48:21-48:50]
- If not fake, use quarterly or semi-annual real deadlines

## Framework 2: Unique Mechanisms for Saturated Markets [11:00-18:04]

When your market has heard it all, repackage your deliverable with a new name:

| Industry | Unique Mechanism Name | What It Actually Is |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Ad Agencies | "Digital Road Maps" | Downsell offers that nurture leads (low-ticket $50-100 item → upsell later) |
| Real Estate | "AI-Based Pre-List System" | Just setters + CRM pre-qualifying leads |
| Coaching | "Geo-Targeted Referrals" | Facebook ads targeting a specific demographic |

**Rule**: "You just create a name for it to make it sound sexy and completely brand new" [9:00-9:07]

**Case study results with unique mechanisms**:
- Ad agency with "Digital Road Maps": $115K spend → 1,045 book calls → $110 CPBC
- Real estate with "AI Pre-List System": $278K spend → 1,400 book calls → $195 CPBC

## Framework 3: Revenue Growth Trajectories (Scaling Roadmap) [18:04-33:07]

### Target KPIs — Cost Per Book Call Benchmarks [18:34-20:04]

| Rating | CPBC | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **High Performing** | <$50 | Very rare outlier. New market or very cheap offer. |
| **Excellent** | $100 | Target. Profit margins through the roof. |
| **Great** | $150 | Solid. |
| **Average** | $200 | Baseline. Most accounts land here. |
| **Low Average** | $300 | Needs work. |
| **Below Average** | $400+ | Terrible funnel/offer. Still can be profitable with high ticket. |

### Target KPIs — Show-Up Rates [20:43-21:54]

| Rating | Rate | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **High Performing** | >80% | Top 1 percentile. Requires heavy personalization: voice notes, texts, calls, loom videos. |
| **Average** | >65% | Standard. |
| **Underperforming** | <50% | Problem. Half your ad spend is wasted. |

**How to improve show-up rates**: text, call, email, personalized Loom, specific sequences — all BEFORE the call. [21:01-21:54]

### Target KPIs — Close Rates [22:03-23:26]

| Rating | Rate | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **High Performing** | >60% | Crushing it. |
| **Average** | 25-33% | Baseline. |
| **Underperforming** | <15% | Wasting money. |

### Revenue Growth Equation [20:25-20:40]
```
Revenue = (Lower CPBC) × (Higher Show-Up Rate) × (Higher Close Rate)
```
"If you have all three together, you're very very profitable on day one." [20:38-20:43]

### Trajectory: <$35K/mo Revenue [23:43-27:18]
- Recommended spend: $2,000/mo
- Coaching program: $4,500 average
- Close rate: 33% average
- Even with $400 CPBC (terrible funnel): 5 calls → 1 close → $4,500 revenue from $2K spend
- Average ($150 CPBC): 13 calls → 4 closes → $18K revenue from $2K spend
- Best case ($50 CPBC): 40 calls → 13 closes → $54K revenue from $2K spend

### Trajectory: $100K/mo Revenue [27:49-29:35]
- Recommended spend: $7,500/mo

### Trajectory: $500K/mo Revenue [29:50-33:07]
- Recommended spend: $60,000/mo ($2K/day minimum)
- **Critical change**: Raise price to $12K minimum (up from $4.5K)

- "More clients = more staff = margins drop below 25%. Not sexy." [31:02]

## Framework 4: Top Landing Pages / Funnels 2024 [34:02-44:35]

### Core Truth: "One winning funnel is a myth" [34:15-35:02]
- "If you see a guru preaching this is the best funnel, that's complete shit. On the back end they're testing 4-6 different funnels simultaneously." [61:00-61:05]
- Split test 3-4 funnels simultaneously with equal budget
- Within 2 weeks you'll know the winning funnel for YOUR business
- After finding winner: allocate 80% of budget to it [61:43-61:49]

### Funnel 1: Video Sales Letter (VSSL) [35:22-39:42]

Structure:
```
1. Market Callout + Offer + New Opportunity + Guarantee
   → Answers: "How is this different? What's in it for me?"
2. Proof of Claims (Case Studies) — unlimited on VSSL, max 3 in ads
3. First CTA (Book a Call)
4. Unique Mechanism (50% of script length in unsaturated markets)
5. More Proof / Case Studies
6. Final CTA
```

**For saturated markets**: Focus LESS on unique mechanism, MORE on answering the 6 questions [38:86-39:42]

### The 6 Questions Every Funnel Must Answer [39:42-40:58]
*(Credited to Kaiback)*

1. **How is this different?**
2. **What's in it for me?**
3. **How do I know this is real?** (case studies)
4. **What's holding me back?** (address objections)
5. **How does it work?** (unique mechanism section)
6. **What do I have to lose?** (guarantee)

### Funnel 2: Deck Sales Letter (DSL) [35:30-40:22]
- Same as VSSL but in slide format
- Less drop-off from click to book call vs video
- "Retention spans decreasing due to TikTok. DSL works for people like me who can't watch 30 seconds." [35:38-35:51]
- "Doesn't have to look sexy to convert" [41:06-41:08]

### Funnel 3: Lead Form → VSSL/DSL → Calendly [41:11-43:13]
- **Most powerful for pre-qualification**
- Lead form is native Facebook integration (auto-filled = low friction)
- Conditional logic question first: "Are you [ICP]? Yes/No"
  - Yes → proceed to next questions → VSSL/DSL → Calendly
  - No → lead thrown away + NOT seeded in pixel
- Collect: name, phone, email before they even see the funnel
- Setters follow up via voice notes, SMS, email, calls
- Show-up rate: 75%+ achievable with this system
- *Source: [41:11-43:13]*

### Funnel 4: Conditional Logic (Critical for 2024) [43:13-44:35]
- Seasons the pixel: Facebook learns what a "yes" (qualified) person looks like
- Creates a virtuous cycle: qualified leads → seasoned pixel → more qualified leads → lower CPBC
- "If you don't have conditional logic, you're burning money" [43:39-43:44]
- Can use multiple choice (not just yes/no): coach, consultant, lead gen, e-commerce → disqualify non-ICP
- *Source: [43:13-44:35]*

## Framework 5: Philosophy for Scale [49:44-62:00]

### Creative Testing Framework [49:44-52:29]
- When spending $10K+/day: need 20-30 new creatives per week
- Naming convention system is CRITICAL for scale:
  - **Video**: VV (VV1, VV2, VV36...)
  - **Image**: IMG (IMG1, IMG2...)
  - **Carousel**: C (C1, C8...)
  - **GIF**: GIF (GIF35...)
  - **Ad Copy**: ADC (ADC1, ADC5...)
  - **Landing Page**: LP (LP1=VSSL, LP2=DSL, LP3=Calendly, LP4=Optin→VSSL)
- "This is how I enabled Amazon to scale, Jordan Platin, Miracle brand" [51:37-51:42]
- Each ad's full name = creative code + ad copy code + landing page
- Relay performance to creative team via shared tracker spreadsheet
- *Source: [49:44-52:29]*

### Broader Is Better (2024 Targeting) [53:27-55:57]
- Post iOS 14: Facebook lost 97% of data. Bigger audience = better performance.
- Rules for 2024:
  - 18+ (no age narrowing)
  - Both male and female
  - All languages
  - Advantage+ targeting ON
  - US outperforms smaller geos (330M people vs 20-50M)
- "I've split tested this. Same ads, same offers, same funnels. Broader always wins." [55:00-55:10]
- Pre-qualify through lead forms, NOT through targeting
- *Source: [53:27-55:57]*

### Advantage+ Enhancements = OFF [56:50-58:14]
- Advantage+ shopping campaign structure = ON (outperforms traditional CBOs 90%+ of the time)
- Advantage+ enhancements (auto sound, voiceovers, text overlays) = "really really bad, off-brand" → turn OFF
- "They want you to put music. No. Definitely not." [58:10-58:14]

### Individual Ads First, Then Dynamics [58:33-59:36]
1. Test 10 new videos individually (1 per ad set)
2. Find 4 winners (BPAs — Best Performing Ads)
3. Put winners into a Dynamic creative ad set for longevity
4. Dynamic acts like CBO — allocates 90% budget to best performer
- "Single → find BPAs → put in Dynamics for longevity" [59:31-59:36]

### One Variable at a Time [59:36-60:33]
- When AB testing: change ONLY one variable
- Testing landing pages? Use same best creative + best ad copy + best headline + best offer
- "If one slight thing is changing, you accurately pick that out. Your AB test is very accurate." [60:14-60:26]

### Tracking & Dashboards [50:06-50:28]
- "If it's not tracked, you're probably losing money" [50:11]
- Track weekly + daily:
  - Cost per book call
  - CPL (cost per lead)
  - Close rates
  - Show-up rates
- Custom dashboard: CRM → spreadsheet + Facebook Ads → spreadsheet (cross-reference)
- "Facebook sometimes shows 100 book calls but CRM shows 75. Cross-reference." [14:54-15:08]
- For high spend ($200-300K/mo): use Hyros attribution tracking
- For lower spend: hire dev to build custom dashboard connecting Zapier → CRM → spreadsheet
- *Source: [14:20-15:20, 50:06-50:28]*

## Operator SOPs Extracted

### Ad Creative Operator — Offer Equation SOP
1. Write market callout (name ICP directly)
2. State pain point (their #1 struggle)
3. Present solution (branded method name)
4. Include 3 case studies max (from → to, with timeframe)
5. Name your unique mechanism (sexy name for deliverable)
6. Add real deadline urgency (not fake timers)
7. CTA to book call or watch VSL/DSL

### Ad Operations Operator — Scaling SOP
1. Set CPBC benchmarks: target $100-150, accept up to $300, alarm at $400+
2. Track show-up rate: target >65%, alarm <50%
3. Track close rate: target >33%, alarm <15%
4. Revenue formula: Lower CPBC × Higher Show-Up × Higher Close Rate
5. Scale rule: At $500K/mo, raise price to $12K+, reduce client count

### Funnel Testing Operator — Split Test SOP
1. Launch 3-4 funnels simultaneously with EQUAL budget
2. Funnels to test: VSSL, DSL, Lead Form→VSSL, Direct Calendly
3. Run for 2 weeks minimum
4. Winner gets 80% of total budget
5. Continue testing remaining 20% on new funnels
6. "One winning funnel is a myth" — re-test every 2 weeks

### Lead Qualification Operator — Conditional Logic SOP
1. First question: "Are you [ICP]?" Yes/No
2. If No → discard lead, don't seed pixel
3. If Yes → collect name, phone, email (auto-filled via Facebook)
4. Direct to VSSL/DSL then Calendly
5. Setters follow up: voice notes, SMS, email, calls
6. Target: 75%+ show-up rate
