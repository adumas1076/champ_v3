## Core Framework: The 3 C's of Client Onboarding

Every step must achieve these three things [1:05]:

| C | What | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| **Convenience** | Zero friction for the client. Graceful swan — kicking underwater, gliding on top. | If it's hard, they'll resent you before you even start. |
| **Clarity** | Crystal clear next steps at every stage. No guessing. | "People are only disappointed when they have the wrong expectations." [15:06] |
| **Confidence** | Client feels MORE confident you can deliver at the END of onboarding than at the start. | Over-deliver from the start. Wow them before results even begin. |

## The Complete Onboarding Flow

### Step 1: Get the Yes (on the sales call) [0:37]
- Presumptive close — assume they're 100% going ahead
- Start onboarding process REGARDLESS of whether they've paid yet
- *Source: [0:37-1:05]*

### Step 2: Book the Onboarding Call (on the sales call) [2:16]
- **Non-negotiable:** Book it BEFORE you leave the sales call
- Do NOT send a link and say "find a time" — they'll get distracted
- This is Hormozi's BAMFAM: Book A Meeting From A Meeting
- Target: within 24-72 hours of the close
- *Source: [2:16-2:54]*

### Step 3: Fill the Trigger Form (immediately after booking) [2:54]
- Simple form (Google Form or GoHighLevel): 3-6 questions
- Captures: company name, email, address, price, package, service
- This form TRIGGERS the entire automation
- Last manual step — everything after is automated
- *Source: [2:54-3:40]*

### Step 4: Auto-Send Contract + Invoice (triggered instantly) [3:42]
- **Contract:** Terms, service details, price, payment terms, signatures
- **Invoice:** NOT a payment link — a proper invoice (accounting done right from day 1)
- Both sent simultaneously, automatically
- *Source: [3:42-5:03]*

### Step 5: Payment Follow-Up (automated) [5:03]
- 24-hour check: have they paid?
- If NO → 3-step email follow-up sequence (automated)
- If still NO after 3 emails → Slack notification to team for manual follow-up call
- If YES → add tag "action-invoice-1-paid" → triggers next steps
- *Source: [5:03-6:11]*

### Step 6: Send Gift (automated, triggered by payment) [6:16]
- **Non-negotiable step.** Send a physical gift.
- Platten uses non-alcoholic hampers (~$100)
- Include a personalized note
- Automated: payment tag → email to supplier with client address → gift ships
- "When was the last time a service provider sent you a gift? Exactly."
- For very high-ticket: Mont Blanc pens
- *Source: [6:16-7:37]*

### Step 7: Create Group Chat (instant, before payment) [7:57]
- WhatsApp preferred (closest to personal communication)
- Include: client + closer + account manager + media buyers + designers — everyone working on the account
- **Critical: Do this BEFORE they've paid.** Don't wait for payment.
- Why: Creates social pressure to follow through. Team responds with excitement (fire emojis, "can't wait to help you scale")
- This "mops up" fence-sitters who said yes but haven't committed yet
- *Source: [7:57-10:17]*

### Step 8: Welcome Message (automated, in group chat) [10:19]
Four components, must fit in ONE phone screenshot:
1. **Hype** — Reaffirm the result you're going to achieve for them
2. **Intro the team** — Who they'll be working with
3. **Confirm the call** — Onboarding call date/time
4. **Send the onboarding page** — Link to client dashboard (not full dashboard yet — just the form page)
- *Source: [10:19-13:06]*

### Step 9: The Onboarding Call (24-72 hours after close) [13:40]
Five steps on this call:

| # | Step | What You Do | Source |
|---|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **Set Expectations** | Outline the roadmap: what happens over 3/6/12 months. UNDER-promise. "If it takes 2 weeks to launch, tell them that." | [14:21] |
| 2 | **Finish Onboarding Form** | Review their form, fill gaps, get technical access (ad accounts, etc.) | [15:31] |
| 3 | **Reveal the Client Dashboard** | The wow moment — branded Notion dashboard with roadmap, form, file storage, call recordings | [18:43] |
| 4 | **Next Steps** | Crystal clear: "We're going to build X, then present it to you on the kickoff call on [date]" | [16:55] |
| 5 | **Book the Kickoff Call** | Book it before leaving the call. BAMFAM again. | [17:27] |

- Onboarding call = 1-on-1 (not group). Kickoff call = 1-to-many (team present).
- *Source: [13:40-18:33]*

### Step 10: The Client Dashboard (Notion) [19:11]
Four components:

| # | Component | What It Contains |
|---|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Roadmap** | Simple table: what happens over next few months. One place to align. |
| 2 | **Onboarding Form** | Questions + access instructions (ad accounts, etc.) |
| 3 | **File Storage** | Google Drive link for images, videos, assets |
| 4 | **Call Recordings** | Auto-imported from Google Meets. Client can replay any call. |

- Branded: client's logo, cover image, name at top
- Automated via Make.com + Notion
- "We've had so many clients comment on the professionalism this instills" [21:22]
- *Source: [19:11-23:07]*

### Step 11: First Delivery (between onboarding and kickoff) [24:10]
- Build the first tangible deliverable (ads, strategy, figma, etc.)
- Present on the kickoff call — get client feedback before launching
- Even if you know what works, involve them: "No nasty surprises, no awkward conversations"
- *Source: [24:10-24:53]*

### Step 12: The Kickoff Call (up to 5 days after onboarding) [24:47]
Three steps:
1. **Answer questions** — "Before we get into this, any questions since we last spoke?"
2. **Reintroduce team** — Physical introductions on video. Name, role, what they do on this account specifically.
3. **Present first stage strategy** — "This is what we're launching. Thoughts? Feedback?"
- Onboarding = 1-on-1. Kickoff = 1-to-many (team present).
- Kickoff recording auto-feeds into the client dashboard
- *Source: [24:47-27:19]*
