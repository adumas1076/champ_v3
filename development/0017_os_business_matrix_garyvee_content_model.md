## Core Philosophy: Document, Don't Create [0:40]

> "The reason why that's important is because that's going to give you the most effective way to hit the amount of volume of content that you want to be posting." [0:47-0:52]

**Document** = capture what you're already doing (meetings, calls, work, thoughts)
**Create** = sit down and try to make something from nothing

Documenting is sustainable. Creating burns you out. The content model is built on documentation.

## The GaryVee Content Model — The Reverse Pyramid

### The Framework (from garyvaynerchuk.com)

> "My content strategy is predicated on a 'reverse pyramid' model. It starts with a piece of 'pillar content.' My team is able to repurpose that one piece of content into dozens of smaller pieces of content, contextual to the platforms that we distribute them to."

```
        PILLAR CONTENT
       (1 long-form piece)
            │
    ┌───────┼───────┐
    ▼       ▼       ▼
  Round 1: Long-form distribution
  (YouTube, Facebook, IGTV, Podcast, Blog)
            │
    ┌───────┼───────┐
    ▼       ▼       ▼
  Round 2: Micro content (2-4 min clips)
  (Facebook, Instagram, contextual edits)
            │
    ┌───────┼───────┐
    ▼       ▼       ▼
  Round 3: Micro-micro content
  (60s clips, 30s clips, <30s clips,
   images, graphics, quotes, memes)
  (Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, Snapchat, TikTok)
```

## The 4-Step Process

### Step 1: Create Pillar Content [1:23-1:50]

One long-form piece. This is the RAW MATERIAL for everything else.

**Forms it can take:**
- Video (vlog, keynote, Q&A, tutorial)
- Podcast recording
- Phone call / meeting (recorded)
- Live stream
- Written long-form (blog, newsletter)

**Distribute the raw pillar to:**
- YouTube (video)
- Facebook (video)
- IGTV / Instagram Video (video)
- Blog (written transcription)
- Podcast platforms (audio rip)

**Key:** You don't need a film crew. "I'm just making this video on my phone." [2:08-2:15]

*Source: [1:23-2:15] + garyvaynerchuk.com content strategy article*

### Step 2: Listen to What Resonates [2:56-3:04]

> "It doesn't matter what you think. The only thing that matters is what your audience thinks, what the market thinks." [1:16-1:22]

- Read comments
- Check engagement metrics
- See which parts people respond to most
- This DATA informs what you cut next

*Source: [1:16-1:22, 2:56-3:04]*

### Step 3: First Round of Micro Content [3:04-4:07]

Take the pillar and create **2-4 minute clips** based on what resonated.

**How to optimize clips:**
- Add subtitles (most people watch without sound) [3:57-4:00]
- Add a headline/title overlay so scrollers know the topic [3:44-3:53]
- Add a timer [3:41]
- Light editing — "spruce it up" [3:43]

**Distribute to:** Facebook, Instagram

*Source: [3:04-4:07]*

### Step 4: Second Round — Maximum Micro Content [4:08-6:12]

Cut even smaller. This is where volume explodes.

**Formats:**
- 60-second video clips
- 30-second video clips
- <30-second video clips
- Images with quotes
- Graphics / motion graphics
- Audio clips
- Memes
- Screenshot tweets → Instagram posts
- Screenshot notes → Instagram stories
- Carousel posts from best content
- Polls for market research

**Distribute to:** Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Snapchat, TikTok — EVERYWHERE

**Why multiple lengths:** "Hit people at multiple levels. Not everyone has the same attention span." [4:59-5:04]

*Source: [4:08-6:12]*

## Operator SOP — Marketing Operator (Content Volume Engine)

### Weekly Pillar Creation
1. Business owner records ONE pillar piece per week (video, podcast, or meeting)
2. If no recording: operator generates pillar from owner's voice clone + Business Matrix frameworks
3. Pillar content = minimum 15-30 minutes of raw material

### Repurposing Pipeline (per pillar)
4. Distribute pillar as long-form: YouTube, Facebook, podcast, blog
5. Listen: check engagement within 24-48 hours
6. Round 1: Cut 8-10 micro clips (2-4 min) based on best moments
   - Add subtitles (70%+ watch without sound — Lamar stat)
   - Add headline overlay
   - Add timer if applicable
   - Apply Lamar Hook → Lead → Meat → Payoff to each clip
7. Round 2: Cut 15-20 micro-micro clips (60s / 30s / <30s)
   - Apply Every-5 Rule (Lamar)
   - Multiple lengths for different attention spans
8. Round 3: Generate static content
   - Quote graphics from best lines
   - Carousel posts from key points
   - Memes from memorable moments
   - Screenshot tweets/posts for cross-platform
9. Distribute across ALL platforms with platform-specific optimization
10. Track performance → feed insights back into next week's pillar topic

### Content Calendar
11. Post 3-7x per day across all platforms (from the 64+ pieces)
12. Ratio: 50% TOF / 30% MOF / 20% BOF (Lamar content funnel)
13. Every post checked against Lamar 7 mistakes before publishing
14. Monthly review: which topics/formats performed best → double down
