## The Core Four (Lead Generation Channels)

Every lead comes from one of four channels, organized on a 2×2 matrix:

|  | One-to-One (Private) | One-to-Many (Public) |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** (know you) | Warm Outreach | Content Marketing |
| **Cold** (don't know you) | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |

### Channel 1: Warm Outreach
**Who:** People who already know you — friends, followers, past clients, network
**How:**
- Reach out personally (DM, email, call)
- Build rapport FIRST — don't pitch immediately
- Ask for referrals, not direct sales
- "Hey, I'm building something new — know anyone who might be interested?"

**Anthony's Application:** Past clients (Adidas, Telemundo, Skipper, Alpha Wolf), creative network from 20 years, anyone who's seen your work.

### Channel 2: Cold Outreach
**Who:** Strangers who fit your ICP but don't know you
**How:**
- Build targeted lists (list brokers, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo)
- Personalize based on research (3 seconds of personalization > generic blast)
- Minimum 2-3 follow-ups before moving on
- Volume: 100 outreaches per day minimum

**Anthony's Application:** Independent creators on LinkedIn/Instagram making $50K-$300K who are posting about AI frustration.

### Channel 3: Content Marketing (Organic)
**Who:** Anyone who discovers your content
**How:**
- Three-part formula: Grab interest → Keep interest → Satisfy interest
- Every viewer becomes a contactable lead
- 80% education, 20% subtle CTAs
- Volume: 80+ pieces per week (Hormozi's number)

**Anthony's Application:** 3 AI clones + Anthony = 4 faces × 3-5 posts/day × 5 platforms = 60-100 pieces daily.

### Channel 4: Paid Ads
**Who:** Targeted strangers through platform algorithms
**How:**
- Choose platforms matching your demographic
- Use targeting filters + lookalike audiences
- Create ads with: attention hook + value proposition + clear CTA
- Test systematically (see Ad Assembly below)

**Anthony's Application:** Meta Ads + YouTube Ads targeting creators, freelancers, solopreneurs interested in AI.

## Lead Magnet Framework

**Definition:** A free product or service that solves a NARROW but SIGNIFICANT problem, pointing to your core offer.

### 7-Step Creation Process

1. **Define the Problem** — Narrow and specific. Not "grow your business." → "Stop wasting money on AI tools that don't work."
2. **Solve It** — Real, actionable solution. Not "buy my course." → "Here's the exact framework I used to 300x a client's revenue with one AI operator."
3. **Decide Format** — Match the audience:
   | Format | Best For |
   |--------|---------|
   | PDF/Guide | Quick consumption, easy to share |
   | Free Trial | SaaS products (Cocreatiq OS) |
   | Video Training | Complex topics, builds trust |
   | Assessment/Quiz | Engagement, lead qualification |
   | Template/Toolkit | Immediate utility |
   | Mini-Course | Higher perceived value |
4. **Test the Name** — Use the naming formula below
5. **Make It Easy** — 5-15 minutes to consume, not 3 hours
6. **Overdeliver** — More value than expected. "If the free stuff is this good, the paid must be insane."
7. **Include a CTA** — What to do next + reasons to act now

### Lead Magnet Naming Formula
`[Number] + [Adjective] + [Target Audience] + [Desired Outcome] + [Time Frame]`

**Examples:**
- "7 Proven Strategies for Independent Creators to Build $10K/Month Recurring Revenue in 90 Days"
- "The 3-Step Framework That 300x'd a Business Owner's Revenue Using One AI Operator"
- "5 Universal Principles Every Creator Needs Before Touching AI"

### Three Types of Lead Magnets

| Type | What It Does | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Reveal Their Problem** | Assessment, audit, diagnostic | "Is Your Business AI-Ready? Take the 2-Minute Assessment" |
| **Sample/Trial** | Solve a recurring problem for a short time | "Try Cocreatiq OS Free for 14 Days" |
| **Multi-Step Solution** | Give Step 1 of a bigger process | "The First 3 Steps to Staffing Your AI Team (Steps 4-10 are inside the community)" |

## Referral/Affiliate Program Design

**Hormozi's key insight:** Standard 20% commissions generate ZERO discretionary effort. The incentive must be big enough to change behavior.

### Incentive Design
| Tier | Reward | Behavior Change |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Standard | 20% commission | No extra effort (table stakes) |
| Aggressive | 50% first month | People mention you casually |
| Behavior-Changing | 75% + $500 bonus after 10 referrals | People actively recruit for you |

**Test:** "Would someone divert their main work time to unlock this reward?" If no, the incentive is too low.

### Gamification
- Tiered prizes (Bronze/Silver/Gold referrer status)
- Public leaderboards
- Random delivery rewards (slot machine effect — unpredictable bonuses)
- Quick pairing (fast reward after referral converts)
- Public recognition (shoutouts, badges, features)

### Scaling Affiliates
1. Start with 5-10 select affiliates
2. Work out kinks (tracking, payouts, messaging)
3. Use their results as affiliate lead magnets ("Join our affiliate program — Sarah made $3K last month")
4. Scale to broader affiliate recruitment
