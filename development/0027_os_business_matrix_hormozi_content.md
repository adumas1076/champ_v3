## Hook, Retain, Reward — Content Framework

Every piece of content (ad, post, video, email) must have all three:

| Phase | What It Does | Time Window | Example |
|-------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Hook** | Grabs attention, prevents scroll-past | First 3 seconds / first line | "One AI operator. 50% capacity. 300% revenue increase." |
| **Retain** | Keeps them engaged, builds trust | Middle 80% | The story, the framework, the proof |
| **Reward** | Leaves them satisfied, increases future engagement | Last 10% | Actionable takeaway they can use TODAY |

**If you nail the hook but fumble the reward, they never come back.**
**If you nail the reward but fumble the hook, they never see it.**

## The "For You" Framework (5 Tactical Shifts)

How Hormozi restructured content for maximum impact:

| Shift | From | To |
|-------|------|-----|
| **Packaging** | Vague thumbnails | Clear thumbnails communicating explicit value |
| **Intro** | "Hey guys, welcome back" | Proof → Promise → Plan (first 5 seconds) |
| **Content** | B-roll filler | Concrete lists, steps, narratives |

### Repurposing Pipeline
```
1 PILLAR (long-form video, 10-30 min)
  ├── Round 1: 4-8 clips (2-4 min each)
  ├── Round 2: 8-16 clips (60 sec each)
  ├── Round 3: 16-32 clips (<30 sec each)
  ├── Text extracts: 10-20 tweets/posts
  ├── Carousel slides: 3-5 slide sets
  └── Audio: podcast clip, audiogram
  
  TOTAL: 40-80 pieces from ONE pillar
```

### Reverse Pipeline
Best-performing SHORT content → becomes the script for next LONG-form video. The audience tells you what they want more of.

## Ad Assembly Process (Hormozi Ad Testing System)

This is the paid ads equivalent of content repurposing. Systematic testing, not guessing.

### Step 1: Create 6 Angles
An "angle" is the core message/story of the ad. Each angle attacks the problem from a different direction:

| Angle | Example for Cocreatiq |
|-------|----------------------|
| Pain | "You're paying $60K/year for an assistant who works 8 hours. Our operators work 24/7 for $299/month." |
| Proof | "One operator. 50% capacity. 300% revenue increase. Here's the Stripe screenshot." |
| Curiosity | "What if your AI could record itself working and show you the tape?" |
| Authority | "20 years of creative work for Adidas, Telemundo, and Boys & Girls Club — now I'm building AI operators." |
| Contrast | "ChatGPT forgets you exist. Our operators remember how you think." |
| Future | "In 6 months, every business will have AI operators. The question is whether you're first or last." |

### Step 2: Bolt 5 Hooks Onto Each Angle
5 different opening lines per angle = 30 base ads

**Hook Types:**
| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Question | "What if your business ran while you slept?" |
| Stat | "78% of customers buy from the first responder. Can you respond in 60 seconds at 3am?" |
| Story | "Last year I helped a business owner go from struggling to 300% revenue growth..." |
| Contrarian | "Forget AI agents. They're already obsolete." |
| Direct | "I built an AI operator that 300x'd a client's revenue. Here's how." |

### Step 3: Multi-Film (Optional)
Double creative variants (different clothing, setting, lighting) = 60 ads total

### Step 4: Launch
- Each ad in its own ad set
- Force the algorithm to spend on each one (no auto-optimization yet)
- Let each run for validation spend ($20-50 per ad)

### Step 5: Rank and Apply 70/20/10

After validation data comes in, rank all ads top to bottom:

| Budget | What | Purpose |
|--------|------|---------|
| **70%** | Slight variations of top 2 winners | Scale what works (change shirt color, background, minor edit) |
| **20%** | Significant variations of winners | Find new angles (different person, different setting, different hook style) |
| **10%** | Completely new angles with 3 hooks each | Discover next winner before current ones fatigue |

### Step 6: Repeat Weekly
- Kill underperformers every Monday
- Promote new winners every Friday
- Never stop testing — creative fatigue is constant

## Landing Page Conversion

**"80% of optimization happens above the fold."**

### Above-the-Fold Formula
```
HEADLINE (dream outcome in one sentence)
  +
VIDEO or IMAGE (proof, not stock photos)
  +
TESTIMONIALS (social proof, logos, numbers)
  +
CTA BUTTON (one clear action)
```

### Rules
- Test ONE element per week (never multiple)
- Page load under 3 seconds (7% revenue loss per second past 3s)
- Use "incomplete information bias" — blur assets, lock videos until opt-in
- Mobile-first (80%+ of traffic is mobile)

## Key Metric: RPM Over Views

**Track Revenue Per Mille (RPM), not raw views.**

| Metric | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Views | How many people saw it | Vanity — doesn't mean revenue |
| RPM | Revenue generated per 1,000 views | Reality — business owners are high-RPM audiences |

1,000 views from business owners > 100,000 views from teenagers.

## Operator SOP: Content & Ad Production

**When to use:** Daily content production, ad campaign management, or content performance optimization.

### Content Production
1. Create 1 pillar piece per week (long-form, Hook/Retain/Reward structure)
2. Apply Intro Formula: Proof → Promise → Plan
3. Repurpose into 40-80 pieces using the pipeline
4. Schedule across 5 platforms via Content Engine
5. Score each piece against eval criteria (Lamar + Gary Vee frameworks)
6. Feed performance data into autoresearch loop

### Ad Management
1. Build 6 angles for current offer
2. Bolt 5 hooks onto each = 30 ads
3. Launch with validation spend ($20-50 each)
4. After 3-5 days: rank and apply 70/20/10
5. Weekly: kill underperformers, promote winners
6. Track: CPC, CTR, CPL, ROAS
7. Rotate offer/creative when fatigue hits (follow rotation priority list)

**Metrics to track:**
- Content: RPM, engagement rate, follower growth, CTA click-through
- Ads: Cost per lead (CPL), cost per acquisition (CPA), ROAS, creative fatigue rate
